# Inheritance (Working)

> Working document. Not canon.
>
> Inheritance recovers where a generated mechanic continues through a family. It follows Generation. It does not create ontology.

## Three reads

Generation asks:

> What mechanic does this primitive keep making recoverable?

Inheritance asks:

> Where does that mechanic continue, directly or ancestrally?

Dependency asks:

> What must already be true for this term to hold?

Generation, inheritance, and dependency may coincide. They are not assumed to be the same.

## Lens distinction

Generation names the mechanic a source condition makes available.

Inheritance names the continuation of that mechanic through later terms.

Dependency names the immediate supports that must remain true for a term to hold.

A term may:

- depend immediately on one condition,
- inherit ancestrally from another,
- and participate in a generated mechanic that began earlier than either immediate support.

## Lineage reads

Immediate inheritance:

- The nearest recoverable mechanic a term unfolds from.

Ancestral inheritance:

- A deeper mechanic still recognisable through intervening terms.

Dependency:

- The support that must remain present for the term to hold.

Do not collapse these.

## Discipline

Inheritance is not taxonomy.

Taxonomy groups terms by likeness.

Inheritance recovers the mechanic a family continues to carry from its generator.

A term may depend on one condition while inheriting a mechanic from another.

## Recovery rule

Recover upward before outward.

1. Recover the generator.
2. Recover the mechanic.
3. Test where the mechanic continues.
4. Distinguish immediate inheritance, ancestral inheritance, and dependency.
5. Recover the family.
6. Only then recalibrate individual notes.

If Generation remains open, Inheritance remains provisional.

# Relation → Carrier family

Generator:

- Relation

Generated mechanic:

- Open. Current pressure from Generation: Relation may generate placeability / structural answerability more directly than the whole Carrier family.

Immediate inheritance:

- Place appears immediately held by Relation.
- Carry and Trace appear immediately generated by Connection as directional passage.

Ancestral inheritance:

- Carry and Trace may still inherit Relation ancestrally through Connection and Hold.

Dependencies:

- Carry and Trace depend on Hold and Connection.
- Place depends on Relation.

Appears through:

- Hold
- Carry
- Trace
- Read
- Place

Observed reads:

- Hold and Carry show dependency without sequence.
- Carry and Trace read forward and backward availability through held connection.
- Relation directly carries Place.
- Connection directly carries Carry and Trace.

Pressure:

- Carrier terms may be note-grammar operators as well as inherited mechanics.
- Trace, Read, and Place need to be checked as inherited mechanics, not merely note-grammar operations.
- Relation → Carrier is too compressed if Carry/Trace are immediately generated by Connection.

Must recover:

- What from Relation remains recognisable in every carrier term.
- Whether Carrier family should split into Place / Hold / Connection-passage / Read operations.

Decision:

- Open.

# Availability → Participation family

Generator:

- Availability

Generated mechanic:

- Current pressure from Generation: presentability / enterability.

Immediate inheritance:

- Presence may be immediate local availability of Coupling.
- Participation appears immediately generated by Carrying + Presence + Bearing Relation entering active scope.

Ancestral inheritance:

- Participation may inherit Availability ancestrally through Presence.

Dependencies:

- Presence depends on Coupling.
- Bearing Relation depends on Coupling, Presence, and Bearing.
- Participation depends on Carrying, Presence, and Bearing Relation.

Appears through:

- Presence
- Bearing Relation
- Participation
- Participant
- Member

Observed reads:

- Presence names coupled relation locally available to read.
- Bearing Relation names coupled relation bearing on coupled relation.
- Participation names a bearing relation actively taking part in a scope.

Pressure:

- Participation is not simply Place-family.
- Availability → Participation is too compressed if Availability generates presentability and Participation activates a present bearing relation.
- Participant and Member should not be tested until Participation is better placed.

Must recover:

- What from Availability remains recognisable through Presence, Bearing Relation, Participation, Participant, and Member.
- Whether Availability should be treated as ancestral rather than immediate in Participation.

Decision:

- Open.

# Connection → Coupling family

Generator:

- Connection

Generated mechanic:

- Current pressure from Generation: passage.

Immediate inheritance:

- Carry and Trace appear immediately generated by Connection as directional availability.
- Coupling appears immediately generated by Resolved Asymmetry + Carrying rather than by Connection.

Ancestral inheritance:

- Coupling may inherit Connection ancestrally through Carrying and resolved relation.
- Nesting may inherit through Coupling rather than directly from Connection.

Dependencies:

- Connection depends on Relation.
- Carry / Trace depend on Connection and Hold.
- Coupling depends on Resolved Asymmetry and Carrying.

Appears through:

- Carry
- Trace
- Coupling
- Couple
- Nest
- Nesting

Observed reads:

- Carry and Trace are two directions through one connection.
- Coupling holds distinct resolved conditions in mutual availability without collapse.
- Couple:Nest and Coupling:Nesting may not name the same ratio.
- Noun forms may mark gathered positions while -ing forms mark proceeding operations.

Pressure:

- Connection → Coupling family is too compressed if Connection generates passage and Coupling generates mutual availability.
- Nesting may inherit Connection ancestrally but Coupling immediately.

Must recover:

- Whether Coupling and Nesting inherit one connection mechanic, or whether Nesting marks a later mechanic generated by Coupling.
- Whether the Connection family should be re-read as passage rather than coupling.

Decision:

- Open.

## Guard

Do not make a family canonical because it reads well.

Accept an inheritance only where the Atlas repeatedly recovers the same mechanic across the family.

If a member fails the read, decide whether the family is wrong, the generator is wrong, the dependency is being confused for inheritance, or the note has drifted.

The Atlas is the test. The working read yields.
