# Time

Held clearance as interval and continuation — relation persisting without collapsing into absolute self-sameness.

Downstream, time becomes readable as the rate and scale at which an order resolves. Different systems do not have different kinds of time first; they resolve held clearance at different rates and scales of order.

Recurring relation can make time measurable without producing Time. At human scale, temporal awareness is resolution-dependent: continuity may be carrying without being distinctly noticed, while [[Return]] provides a boundary at which that continuity becomes readable again. [[Cycle]] bounds the return, and [[Frequency]] counts returns per interval. These are downstream human-scale reads of Time, not replacements for its first-order placement.

## Places

Time places held clearance as interval and continuation.

## Holds

Time is held by [[Clearance]]. Relation must remain apart enough to continue without collapsing into static sameness.

## Pairs

Time pairs with [[Space]]. Time reads clearance as interval and continuation; Space reads clearance as separation and extent.

## Traces

- [[Clearance]]

## Nests

Time nests before chronology, sequence, process, progression, and causation. Those are later traversal reads of time, not the first condition.

## Reads

Time becomes recognisable where held clearance can be read as interval and continuation — where relation is present as persisting rather than as static sameness.

Humanly readable time changes with scale and resolution. When carrying crosses a boundary that a person can resolve — a beat, breath, pause, wait, interruption, completed operation, or recognised return — continuation becomes temporally distinct. When those boundaries fall below resolution or are carried implicitly, continuity may remain active while elapsed time becomes less noticeable.

This does not mean humans create Time or only encounter it through regular cycles. It means temporal awareness depends on which differences and returns can enter the participant's current resolution band. When an order must be stepped through explicitly, sequence is visible and time may feel slow or granular. Where learning or sustained participation carries more of the order implicitly, sequence becomes less visible and time may feel more fluid.
