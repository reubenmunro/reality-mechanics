# Strained Asymmetry

Bounded asymmetry under contrast — the condition through which localised unevenness becomes consequential.

Strained Asymmetry is held by [[Bounded Asymmetry]]. Local unevenness must be distinguishable before it can be strained.

## Places

Strained Asymmetry places bounded asymmetry under contrast.

## Holds

Strained Asymmetry is held by [[Bounded Asymmetry]]. Local unevenness must be distinguishable before it can be strained.

## Pairs

Strained Asymmetry pairs with [[Resolved Asymmetry]]. Strained asymmetry names the contrast; resolved asymmetry names the same contrast determined at scope.

## Traces

- [[Bounded Asymmetry]]

## Nests

Strained Asymmetry nests between bounded asymmetry and resolved asymmetry. It echoes [[Strain]] without becoming the whole operational condition.

## Reads

Strained asymmetry becomes recognisable where bounded asymmetry can be read as under contrast — where the localised unevenness is present enough that something must either hold or resolve.
