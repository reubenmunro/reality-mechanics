# Bounded Asymmetry

The hinge where uneven relation becomes local enough to be distinguished — asymmetry held locally enough that a boundary can form.

Bounded Asymmetry is held by [[Asymmetry]]. Unevenness must remain readable while it is held locally.

## Places

Bounded Asymmetry places the hinge where uneven relation becomes local enough to be distinguished.

## Holds

Bounded Asymmetry is held by [[Asymmetry]]. Unevenness must remain readable while it is held locally.

## Pairs

Asymmetry carries downward. Bounded Asymmetry is the singular condition through which asymmetry becomes local enough to open [[Distinction]] and [[Strained Asymmetry]]. Distinction carries the located and readable branch; Strained Asymmetry carries the bearing branch.

## Traces

- [[Asymmetry]]
- [[Invariant]]

## Nests

Bounded Asymmetry nests between unlocated unevenness and located distinction. It is the hinge where difference becomes held enough to become locally readable.

## Reads

Bounded asymmetry becomes recognisable where unevenness can be read as local — where asymmetry is present enough for one distinguishable condition to be located against another.

Bounded asymmetry localises asymmetry enough that distinction can form. [[Distinction]] then carries [[Boundary]], through which primitive carriers emerge as the ways located distinction can be carried.

It also makes adaptation possible downstream. Adaptation does not erase asymmetry; it allows local resolutions and shifts to occur while the wider asymmetry remains available for further relation.
