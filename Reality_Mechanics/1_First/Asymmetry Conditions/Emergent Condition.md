# Emergent Condition

Emergent condition names the class of first-order conditions that arise from within relation through asymmetry rather than being imposed from outside it or established by operations. Where operations act on distinction, emergent conditions name what becomes available prior to those acts — the conditions that make operations possible, arising without external cause. Asymmetry, bounded asymmetry, strained asymmetry, and resolved asymmetry are emergent conditions.

## Places

Emergent Condition places the class of first-order conditions that arise from within relation through asymmetry.

## Holds

Emergent Condition is held by [[Asymmetry]]. Uneven relation must be readable before emergence through asymmetry can be named.

## Pairs

Emergent Condition pairs with [[Operational Condition]]. Emergent conditions arise through asymmetry; operational conditions act on located distinction.

Emergent Condition nests asymmetry, bounded asymmetry, strained asymmetry, and resolved asymmetry as conditions that become available before imposed operation.
