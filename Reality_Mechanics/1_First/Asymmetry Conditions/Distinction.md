# Distinction

Bounded unevenness becoming distinguishable within relation before it is located as boundary or read as difference.

Distinction is held by [[Bounded Asymmetry]]. Asymmetry must be local enough for this and not-this to become distinguishable before distinction can be named.

[[Asymmetry]] makes distinction possible. [[Bounded Asymmetry]] makes the unevenness local enough to distinguish. Distinction names that distinguishability itself. [[Boundary]] then locates it; [[Difference]] later names its produced read as non-sameness.

## Places

Distinction places bounded unevenness becoming distinguishable within relation before it is located as boundary or read as difference.

## Holds

Distinction is held by [[Bounded Asymmetry]]. Asymmetry must be local enough for this and not-this to become distinguishable before distinction can be named.

## Pairs

Distinction is vertical. It is the singular condition from which located distinction and readable non-sameness become available. The asymmetry is expressed in what Distinction carries: [[Boundary]] locates distinction, while [[Difference]] reads distinction as non-sameness.

## Traces

- [[Bounded Asymmetry]]

## Nests

Distinction nests between bounded asymmetry and the conditions that locate or read it. Boundary locates distinction; Difference reads distinction as non-sameness.

## Reads

Distinction becomes recognisable where unevenness within relation can be followed as this and not-this without yet requiring a fixed boundary or a produced read of difference.

Distinction is not division. What is distinguished can remain related, continuous, coupled, or indivisible. Distinction only requires enough unevenness for non-interchangeability to become readable.

- [[Difference]]
