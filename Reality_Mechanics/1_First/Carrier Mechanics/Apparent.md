# Apparent

Distinction as presented enough to become available to notice.

Apparent is held by [[Boundary]], because distinction must be located enough to present.

## Places

Apparent places distinction as presented enough to become available to notice.

## Holds

Apparent is held by [[Boundary]], because distinction must be located enough to present.

## Pairs

Asymmetry carries downward. Apparent is a singular carrier primitive — the condition through which something becomes presented at a boundary. There is no lateral contrast required at the apparent level. The asymmetry is expressed in what apparent makes possible: [[Visible]] and [[Invisible]], [[Notice]] and [[Trace]], [[Apparent Bearing]], and the later source-role read of [[Apparent Source]].

## Traces

- [[Boundary]]

## Nests

Apparent nests before trace, holding, or recognition. It can open visibility, invisibility, notice, and trace without yet becoming any of them.

## Reads

Apparent becomes recognisable where distinction is present enough to be noticed — where something is available to enter notice, attention, and trace without yet being followed, held, or read.
