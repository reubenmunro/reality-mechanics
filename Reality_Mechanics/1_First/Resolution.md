# Resolution

Resolution names bearing determined at the current scope of evaluation. Where [[Bearing]] names strain held as readable, resolution names that held strain settling into a readable condition. What is resolved may remain open to wider trace; resolution closes one condition without ending relation.

Resolution traces from [[Clearance]] because change can only resolve where relation remains held apart enough for difference to be readable. [[Space]] reads that clearance as separation or extent. [[Time]] reads it as interval or continuation. Resolution reads what changes across the held difference.

## Places

Resolution places bearing determined at the current scope of evaluation — held strain settling into a readable condition without terminating relation.

## Holds

Resolution is held by [[Bearing]] and [[Clearance]]. Strain must be held, and relation must remain apart enough for change across the difference to become readable.

## Pairs

No lateral pair is required at this placement. Resolution can shape what is carried without being the source of every carrying.

## Traces

- [[Bearing]]
- [[Clearance]]

## Nests

Resolution nests as the first-order crossing support into Second Order. It closes a condition at one scope without terminating wider relation.

## Reads

Resolution becomes recognisable where bearing can be read as determined: held contrast settled into a readable outcome at the current scope.

Resolution is not simply solving. It is the determination of held difference. Without clearance there is no readable between; without the readable between, there is no interval, separation, or strain across which resolution can occur.

## Order-Terminal

Resolution is order-terminal for First Order. It completes the readable length of first-order asymmetry at the current scope: bearing has become determined enough that the same first-order passage cannot continue as the same passage. What continues must be carried forward, re-entered, or read under another order.
