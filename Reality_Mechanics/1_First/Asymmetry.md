# Asymmetry

Asymmetry names uneven relation. It is the first readable condition that makes distinction possible, and it is not produced by a prior term beyond relation itself. Before boundary, locality, or order can be read, relation must be uneven enough for distinction to begin.

## Places

Asymmetry places the first readable unevenness within [[Relation]].

## Holds

Asymmetry is held by [[Relation]]. Relation must remain held for unevenness within relation to be readable.

## Pairs

Asymmetry pairs with [[Clearance]]: asymmetry gives unevenness, while clearance keeps the relation from collapsing or becoming unrelated.

## Traces

- [[Relation]]

## Nests

Asymmetry nests before boundary and locality. It opens the possibility of distinction without yet locating that distinction.

## Reads

Asymmetry becomes recognisable where relation can no longer be read as uniform spread — where unevenness is present enough for distinction to begin.
