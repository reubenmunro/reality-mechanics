# Availability

The condition through which located distinction can present — what boundary makes possible before carrying, allowance, or resolution are decided.

Availability is held by [[Boundary]]. A distinction must be located before its availability can be read.

## Places

Availability places the condition through which located distinction can present.

## Holds

Availability is held by [[Boundary]]. A distinction must be located before its availability can be read.

## Pairs

Availability pairs with [[Boundary]]: boundary locates distinction; availability names the condition through which that located distinction can present. Each is only locatable against the other — boundary without availability is mere limit; availability without boundary is undirected openness.

## Traces

- [[Boundary]]

## Nests

Availability nests as both a first-order root condition and a passage condition. It names entry into possible participation before carrying, allowance, or resolution are decided.

## Reads

Availability becomes recognisable where a located distinction is present enough to participate in evaluation — where something can enter strain, holding, allowance, or resolution.

**Passage Role.**

Availability is a passage condition as well as a first-order root condition: it names whether a located distinction can enter later carrying without yet naming the carrying itself.
