# Injury

Incompatible carrying within the body at a closure scope — carrying that proceeds against the body's compatible conditions and damages or removes available bearing.

Injury is held by [[Body]], [[Boundary]], [[Carrying]], [[Compatibility]], [[Closure Scope]], [[Harm]], and [[Splinter]]. Carrying must proceed against compatible conditions and a boundary must locate the damage before the condition can be read as injury.

## Reads

Injury becomes readable where embodied carrying is damaged enough that continuing as before would increase incompatibility.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Boundary]] supplies the local embodied limit.

Second: [[Carrying]], [[Compatibility]], and [[Closure Scope]] make damaged participation evaluable.

Third: [[Injury]] places that structural read inside the body branch.

## Carries

Injury carries [[Recovery]]. Recovery names compatible re-bearing following injury — the return of embodied carrying without repeating the incompatibility that caused damage.
