# Physical Space

The structured extent within which physical field conditions become measurable — the dimensional and scaled ground for locating physical distinction.

Physical Space is held by [[Natural World Field]], [[Physical]], [[Physical Field Conditions]], [[Dimension]], and [[Scale]]. Physical field conditions must hold and dimensional structure must be readable before measured extent can be named as physical space.

Physical Space is downstream of [[Space]]. It is not the first-order source of spatial readability.

## Places

## Reads

Physical Space becomes readable where located clearance appears as measurable or modelled physical extent, position, distance, or traversable field relation.

Derivation:

[[Physical]] names natural-world readability as material or field condition.

Physical Space names located clearance read through natural-world material or field conditions.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Relation]], [[Clearance]], [[Space]], [[Relation Read]], and [[Boundary]] make located clearance readable.

Second: [[Dimension]], [[Scale]], and [[Readability]] make spatial relation evaluable by local resolution and mode of read.

Third: [[Physical Space]] places measurable spatial relation inside [[Natural World Field]] and [[Physical Field Conditions]].
