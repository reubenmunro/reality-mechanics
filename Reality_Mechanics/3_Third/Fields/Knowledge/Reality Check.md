# Reality Check

The evaluative read through which a claim, representation, or local model is checked for compatibility with what bears it — the closure-scope test of whether a read remains traceable.

Reality Check is held by [[Reality]], [[Read]], [[Compatibility]], [[Closure Scope]], [[Bearing Source]], [[Dependency Disorder]], and [[Check]]. A claim must be present and compatibility must be evaluable at the relevant closure scope before the check can be performed.

## Pairs

Reality Check pairs with [[Knowledge]]: reality check names the evaluative read through which a claim is checked against what bears it; knowledge names the field where knowing is maintained, tested, and revised. Each requires the other — reality check has no field to apply within without knowledge; knowledge without reality check loses contact with what it claims to know.

## Reads

Reality Check becomes readable where a representation, claim, or read is tested against what can still be borne, resolved, and carried by its bearing source at the relevant closure scope.

Reality Check also tests whether a read is calibrated to the order and scope of the question it is answering. A response may be locally valid inside one order while failing the question posed from another order. This is not simple relativism and not only overclaim; it is a compatibility failure between the resolution offered and the order in which the question requires answerability.

Reality Check also asks whether the boundary that turns read into action remains traceable. A read may appear correct as representation while failing as action if the accountable coupling is hidden, displaced, or treated as if consequence belonged only to the carrier.

Related Practice:

[[Check]] supplies the Atlas practice of locating a boundary by testing from both directions. Reality Check applies checking to whether a read remains compatible with its [[Bearing Source]] at the relevant [[Closure Scope]].

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Bearing]] and [[Resolution]] must hold enough for a read to proceed.

Second: [[Read]], [[Compatibility]], and [[Closure Scope]] make the check evaluable.

Third: [[Reality Check]] applies that evaluation to claims, representations, and branch-local reads.
