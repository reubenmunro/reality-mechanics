# Competence Boundary

The located limit at which a participant, practice, or domain can distinguish what it can compatibly carry from what it cannot — the closure scope of traceable carrying.

Competence Boundary is held by [[Knowledge]], [[Boundary]], [[Compatibility]], [[Reality Check]], [[Bearing Source]], [[Know]], and [[Responsibility]]. Carrying must be traceable, responsibility must remain answerable to its bearing source, and compatibility must be evaluable before the limit of competent carrying can be named.

## Places

Competence Boundary places the located limit at which a participant, practice, or domain can distinguish what it can compatibly carry from what it cannot — the closure scope of traceable carrying.

## Holds

## Pairs

Competence Boundary pairs with [[Modelling]]. Competence Boundary names the located scope at which compatible carrying can no longer be reliably extended; Modelling names the representation through which compatible carrying is extended through medium. Each requires the other: modelling extends what competence boundary locates as the edge of compatible extension.

## Nests

Competence Boundary nests within the Knowledge Field as the closure-scope condition through which traceable carrying distinguishes what is within and beyond its compatible scope.

## Reads

Competence Boundary becomes readable where a participant, practice, or domain can distinguish what can be known, done, claimed, taught, delegated, or taken responsibility for without exceeding the bearing source that can actually carry and check it.
