# Modelling

The condition through which a read, pattern, or form is carried in a medium as a representation — carrying that makes what is read available to recognition without requiring its direct presence.

This note places modelling inside the [[Knowledge Field]] as a path read.

## Places

Modelling places the condition through which a read, pattern, or form is carried in a medium as a representation — carrying that makes what is read available to recognition without requiring its direct presence.

## Holds

Modelling is held by [[Knowledge Field]], [[Read]], [[Recognition Read|Recognition]], [[Pattern]], [[Medium]], and [[Form]]. A read must be present, a pattern must be recognisable, and a medium must carry the form before representation as modelling can be named.

## Pairs

Modelling pairs with [[Competence Boundary]]. Modelling names the representation through which compatible carrying is extended through medium; Competence Boundary names the located scope at which that carrying can no longer be reliably extended. Each requires the other: modelling extends what competence boundary marks as the limit.

## Nests

Modelling nests within the Knowledge Field as the path read for carried representation — the structural ground for AI, externalised cognition, and knowledge-system reads.

## Reads

Modelling becomes readable where a condition is carried in a simplified or usable form without becoming the condition itself.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Boundary]] and [[Availability]] make a condition present enough to be modelled.

Second: [[Read]], [[Recognition Read|Recognition]], [[Pattern]], [[Medium]], [[Form]], and [[Closure Scope]] make the carried model evaluable.

Third: [[Modelling]] places that carried read in the [[Knowledge Field]] as a path read.
