# Inheritance

Inheritance names an our-story path read where memory, practice, term, place, obligation, or relation is carried into later participation.

## Places

Inheritance places the condition through which prior carrying is passed forward through recurrence and recognition — what a field, community, or practice continues to carry from prior relation.

## Holds

Inheritance is held by [[Our Story Field]], [[Carrying]], [[Recurrence]], [[Recognition Read|Recognition]], [[Medium]], [[Compatibility]], [[Kind]], and [[Our Story]]. Prior carrying must be present, recurrence must sustain it, and recognition must make it available before forward-passed carrying can be named as inheritance.

## Pairs

Inheritance pairs with [[Carrying Better]]. Inheritance names the forward-passing of prior carrying; Carrying Better names the condition where retrace makes that inheritance correctable.

## Reads

Inheritance becomes readable where what is received remains identifiable enough to be carried, revised, refused, repaired, or retold.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Bearing]] and [[Availability]] make something carryable beyond the immediate relation.

Second: [[Carrying]], [[Recurrence]], [[Recognition Read|Recognition]], [[Medium]], and [[Compatibility]] make inheritance evaluable.

Third: [[Inheritance]] places that structural read inside the [[Our Story Field]].
