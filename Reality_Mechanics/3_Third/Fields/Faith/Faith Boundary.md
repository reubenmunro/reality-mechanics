# Faith Boundary

Faith Boundary names a carried boundary around relation whose content is not yet structurally readable.

It is above the current read, not above [[Relation]].

## Places

Faith Boundary places the located limit at which relation is recognised but what it carries cannot yet be read — the boundary around unresolvable readability held by faith.

## Holds

Faith Boundary is held by [[Faith Read|Faith]], [[Relation]], [[Boundary]], [[Bearing]], [[Delayed Availability]], and [[Closure Scope]]. Faith must hold the condition and a boundary must locate the limit of current readability before the unresolvable edge can be named.

## Pairs

Faith Boundary pairs with [[Faith Read]]. Faith Boundary names the edge at which faith's claim becomes untestable at scope; Faith Read names the carried commitment that meets that edge.

## Nests

Faith Boundary nests within the Faith Field as the closure-scope condition through which faith holds what cannot yet be read.

## Reads

Faith Boundary becomes readable where relation is recognised, what it is cannot yet be read, and a carried boundary allows bearing before resolution, recurrence, or higher-order return becomes available.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Relation]], [[Boundary]], and [[Bearing]] allow unreadable relation to be recognised and carried without defining its content.

Second: [[Delayed Availability]] and [[Closure Scope]] distinguish unavailable structure from present readability.

Third: [[Faith Boundary]] places this carried boundary inside [[Faith Field]] without making it primitive or source authority.

Movement:

```text
relation is recognised
-> what it is cannot yet be read
-> faith carries a boundary around unavailable relation
-> bearing continues before resolution
-> recurrence may later make a read available
-> the read may return as recognition, correction, release, or practice
```
