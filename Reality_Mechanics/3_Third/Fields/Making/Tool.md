# Tool

Tool names a making path read where form is made available to extend function through compatible use.

## Places

Tool places the condition through which a made form extends a participant's carrying by translating relation into a usable resolution band — the structural node where form, function, medium, and readability combine to augment what relation can carry.

## Holds

Tool is held by [[Making Field]], [[Form]], [[Function]], [[Medium]], [[Making]], [[Extend]], and [[Readability]]. Form must be carried through making, extension must remain answerable to source, function must be readable through a medium, and the translated condition must enter a usable resolution band before the augmenting condition can be named as a tool.

## Pairs

Tool pairs with [[Hidden Carrying]]. Tool names the produced form that makes carrying available and visible; Hidden Carrying names the condition where produced form displaces and obscures the carrying it depends on.

## Nests

Tool nests within the Making Field as the condition through which made form extends carrying — the structural ground for technology, medium, and instrument reads.

## Reads

Tool becomes readable where a made form allows participation that would otherwise be unavailable, harder to carry, or less compatible by extending carrying into a form the participant can receive, integrate, and carry.

A tool does not remove the participant's resolution band. It changes what reaches it. A microscope translates small-scale structure into optical read. A thermometer translates thermal condition into visible measure. A clock translates ordered recurrence into countable sequence. In each case, the tool extends read by carrying an order outside native resolution into a compatible form.

This makes tool-use answerable to its translation path. A tool can extend read, but it can also fabricate readability where the surface remains usable while the dependency path is hidden, broken, or displaced.

Tool and [[Weapon]] are distinguished by use-path rather than object-kind. Tool extends capacity within answerable purpose. Weapon redirects capacity toward force, harm, threat, disabling, coercion, or override.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Boundary]], [[Availability]], and [[Bearing]] make formed use carryable.

Second: [[Form]], [[Function]], [[Medium]], [[Extend]], [[Readability]], [[Carrying]], and [[Compatibility]] make tool-use evaluable as compatible participation rather than mere access.

Third: [[Tool]] places that structural read inside the [[Making Field]].
