# Self-Bearing

Self-bearing is a constrained compound read linking Self and Bearing.

## Places

Self-Bearing places the condition through which the self carries its own strain — bearing held within the participant's own cognitive carrying rather than displaced or collapsed.

## Holds

Self-Bearing is held by [[Self]], [[Bearing]], [[Carrying]], and [[Compatibility]]. Self must be readable and bearing must be available to the self's own carrying before the held condition can be named as self-bearing.

## Pairs

Self-Bearing pairs with [[Self]]. Self names the bounded cognitive scope; Self-Bearing names the condition where that scope holds and carries its own carrying.

## Traces

- [[Self]]
- [[Bearing]]
- [[Carrying]]
- [[Compatibility]]

## Nests

Self-Bearing nests within the Cognition Field as the condition through which a self holds its own strain in compatible carrying — the structural ground for self-control and self-harm reads.

## Reads

Self-Bearing becomes readable where self-relation is held enough to remain available for compatible carrying.

Derivation:

[[Self]] names recursive distinguishability as a participant.

[[Bearing]] names first-order holding.

Self-Bearing names self-relation held enough to remain available for compatible carrying.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Bearing]] holds available self-relation.

Second: [[Carrying]] and [[Compatibility]] make that self-relation continuable.

Third: [[Self-Bearing]] places that structural read inside cognition.

Higher-Order Edge:

Self-Bearing remains third-order where self-relation is held enough to remain available.

It crosses into higher order where [[Consciousness]] makes the way self-relation is being held available to the participant and [[Self-Regulation]] adjusts that carrying.

## Carries

Self-Bearing carries [[Self-Control]] and [[Self-Harm]]. Self-Control names the applied read where self-bearing regulates the carrying of an impulse; Self-Harm names the applied read where self-bearing fails and carrying turns against the self's own conditions.
