# Self

The condition through which cognition locates a boundary that distinguishes this participant's carrying from others — the recognised closure scope through which identity becomes locally readable.

Self is held by [[Cognition]], [[Boundary]], [[Identity]], [[Recognition Read|Recognition]], and [[Closure Scope]]. Cognition must be present, a boundary must distinguish this carrying scope, and recognition must make identity locally readable before the condition can be named as self.

## Places

Self pairs with [[Self-Bearing]]. Self names the bounded cognitive scope; Self-Bearing names the condition where that scope actively holds its own carrying.

## Nests

Self nests within the Cognition Field as the condition through which cognition locates its own carrying boundary — the structural ground for self-bearing, self-control, and self-harm reads.

## Reads

Self becomes readable where relation is locally and recursively distinguishable as a participant across a closure scope.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Boundary]] locates distinguishability within relation.

Second: [[Identity]] and [[Recognition Read|Recognition]] allow recursive distinguishability to remain readable at [[Closure Scope]].

Third: [[Self]] places that recursive read inside the cognition branch.

Higher-Order Edge:

Self remains third-order where the participant is readable as self at a closure scope.

It crosses into higher order only where the self-read returns into its own carrying through [[Consciousness]], [[Recursion]], [[Self-Modelling]], or [[Self-Regulation]].

## Carries

Self carries [[Self-Bearing]]. Where a boundary distinguishes this participant's carrying scope, self-bearing names the condition through which that scope holds and carries its own strain.
