# Violation

Violation names a coupled boundary being crossed, overridden, or made unavailable without compatible participation. Boundary change becomes violation where it is carried against the support that the relationship or bearing relation requires.

## Places

Violation places the condition of a coupled boundary broken against compatible participation — where what was required for bearing to continue is overridden.

## Holds

Violation is held by [[Coupled Boundary]] and [[Compatibility]]. A boundary must be coupled and compatibility between participants must be required before a crossing can be read as violation rather than ordinary boundary change.

## Pairs

Violation pairs with [[Repair]]. Violation names the break of a coupled boundary against participation; Repair names the restoration of what the break damaged.

## Traces

- [[Coupled Boundary]]
- [[Compatibility]]

## Nests

Violation nests within Relational Participation as a bearing-relations read on boundary conditions. It names where the structural requirement of compatible participation is overridden.

## Reads

Violation becomes readable where a coupled boundary is crossed or altered in a way that damages available bearing, support, or retraceable participation.

## Carries

- [[Betrayal]]
- [[Repair]]
- [[Harm]]
- [[Domination]]
