# Control After Harm

Control After Harm reads a local case where control enters after damaged bearing and must be checked for protection, repair, or further harm.

## Places

Control After Harm places the applied read where control is exercised following harm — direction of availability in the context of incompatible carrying that preceded it.

## Holds

Control After Harm is held by [[Applied Read]], [[Harm]], [[Control]], [[Repair]], and [[Closure Scope]].

## Pairs

Control After Harm pairs with [[Control Drift]]. Control After Harm names the disorder where control is applied after harm without first restoring bearing; Control Drift names the disorder where control loses retrace to what it was constraining. Each is a distinct form of control losing its structural ground.

## Traces

- [[Applied Read]]
- [[Harm]]
- [[Control]]
- [[Repair]]
- [[Closure Scope]]

## Nests

Control After Harm nests within Relational Participation as an applied read of Control following harm, where direction of availability must be read against the incompatible carrying that preceded it.

## Reads

Control After Harm becomes readable where direction enters after harm and must preserve compatible re-bearing rather than force proceeding.

The dependency movement is:

First: [[Bearing]] and [[Resolution]] must be re-entered after harm.

Second: [[Compatibility]], [[Repair]], and [[Closure Scope]] make control evaluable as protection, repair support, or forced continuation.

Third: [[Control After Harm]] tests that condition inside the [[Relational Participation Field]] as an applied relation read.

Case Movement:

```text
harm damages bearing
-> control enters as protection
-> repair is required
-> control either supports re-bearing or forces continuation
```
