# Authority

The read that a participant, claim, practice, or structure has warranted standing to direct or determine continuation within a bounded scope.

Authority is held by [[Boundary]], [[Responsibility]], [[Bearing Source]], [[Control]], and [[Competence Boundary]]. Standing must be scoped, warranted, answerable, direction-capable, and limited before authority can be named.

Authority is not control, responsibility, recognition, or competence alone. Control is direction exercised. Authority is the warranted standing to exercise direction or make a determination. Responsibility is answerability for what follows. Competence Boundary locates the limit of that standing. Bearing Source makes the warrant retraceable.

## Places

## Nests

Authority nests within a declared scope where standing can be checked against its warrant, answerability, direction, and limit.

## Reads

Authority becomes readable where a participant, claim, practice, or structure can answer why it may direct or determine continuation here, within what boundary, and up to what competence limit.

Recognition, clearance, or recarry may participate in establishing authority in a particular practice, but none is a universal prerequisite. Authority does not become warranted merely because it is recognised or exercised.
