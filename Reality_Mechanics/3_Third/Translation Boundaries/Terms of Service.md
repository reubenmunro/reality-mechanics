# Terms of Service

Terms of Service names the translation boundary where human service becomes terminal to the reality terms under which participation remains carried by the earth. In ordinary use, the phrase names contractual conditions for using a platform. In the Atlas, it can be read more basically: the first service is [[Earth Carrying]], and its terms are the conditions under which human participation remains possible, grounded, corrected, and retraceable.

## Places

Terms of Service places the boundary where human service must be translated back to the earth carrying it.

## Holds

Terms of Service is held by [[Earth Carrying]], [[Carrying]], [[Term]], [[Trace]], [[Participation]], and [[Translation Boundary]].

## Pairs

Terms of Service pairs with [[Terms and Conditions]]. Both reverse the ordinary acceptance order: participation must remain answerable to the terms that make carrying possible. Terms of Service holds that reversal outward, where human service answers to the earth carrying it; Terms and Conditions holds it inward, where terms serve inside the Atlas through traceable conditions. Each requires the other to be locatable.

## Nests

Terms of Service nests within Translation Boundaries as the service-to-earth terminal read: a human arrangement reaches the field that carries it and must be read by that field's terms.

## Reads

Terms of Service becomes recognisable where human systems meet the carrying conditions they depend on — where participation does not merely accept written terms inside a platform, but must retrace whether its own terms still answer to the earth relation carrying it.
