# Nesting

Ordered participation — prior organisation remaining available within later organisation.

Nesting is held by [[Participation]], [[Recurrence]], and [[Field of Participation]]. Participation must recur in a field before prior organisation can remain available within later organisation.

Nesting carries; modelling represents.

A prior read, trace, or organisation becomes nested where it continues to participate inside a later read without needing to be separately reconstructed.

Nested relation orders. Coupling makes relation connected enough to participate; nesting keeps that participation dependency-ordered enough to remain retraceable. Connection without nesting becomes flat; nesting without coupling becomes detached.

Nesting is what makes a term's position within the dependency order determinable. Without nesting, carry and trace give direction but not depth — a term can be followed forward or backward, but where it sits in the order can only be asserted, not located. Where nesting holds, running carry and trace simultaneously through the same passage resolves position: the nested structure is what the carry-trace test is actually measuring. This is the structural warrant for nesting — not only that it keeps participation retraceable, but that it makes the order locatable at all.

## Nests

Nesting nests where prior organisation remains available within later organisation without being replaced.

## Reads

Nesting becomes recognisable where later participation carries prior organisation within it, ordering relation by dependency rather than flattening it into connection alone.

The prior path is not repeated as a copy. It remains available within the new participation as a carried structure.

**Construction read.** A beam is supported by a foundation. Tracing back from the beam and carrying forward from the foundation do not arrive at each other directly — they meet at a stud or fixing nested between them. The nested term is where both directions resolve. From the beam alone, the support is present but how it is held is not fully readable. From the foundation alone, the load path is present but its terminus is not. Running both directions simultaneously locates the stud — the nesting position — and makes its bearing readable: what it is holding, what presses on it, what it must withstand. Constraint is one form that bearing takes when readable as a limit, but it is downstream of the bearing the nesting reveals, not the nesting itself. Once the wall is clad, the fixing becomes invisible; the load still carries, the beam still holds, but the bearing at the nested term is no longer directly readable. The nesting remains structurally present and recoverable by the carry-trace test — but it has become hidden bearing.
