# Extractive Acceleration

The applied diagnosis of acceleration produced by converting long-order Earth carrying into short-order resolution.

Extractive Acceleration is held by [[Applied Read]], [[Diagnosis]], [[Earth Carrying]], [[Acceleration]], [[Pace]], [[Resolution]], [[Carrying]], [[Scale]], and [[Source Drift]]. The long-order carrier, the fast-order resolution, the supportable pace, and the scale transition must all be readable before acceleration can be diagnosed as extractive rather than treated as neutral speed.

Earth carries long-order sequence: geological formation, ecological renewal, stored energy, soils, water cycles, inherited trust, bodies, attention, and repair conditions. Extractive systems draw those slow orders into fast activity. The fast order then appears efficient, productive, or inevitable while the slower carrier is treated as background.

This is not a claim that time itself accelerates. It is a diagnosis of resolution-rate inside a coupled relation.

Modern speed can therefore be read as long-order carrying converted into short-order resolution.

The diagnostic question is not only whether a system can go faster. It is what order is paying for the faster resolution, and whether that order can be restored. A system may appear progressive while becoming less supportable, because it resolves by spending carriers it cannot regenerate at the same order.

## Nests

Extractive Acceleration nests within Applied Diagnosis as a scale-and-carrying diagnosis. It becomes visible where a fast order appears self-accelerating because the slower order carrying it has been backgrounded.

Compression and nesting can look the same from inside the accelerated order. A system may appear to be doing the same sequence faster, while a slower nested carrier is being drawn down to make that speed possible. The distinction becomes readable only when the carrying source is retraced.

## Reads

Extractive Acceleration becomes readable where slow-order carrying is converted into fast-order resolution, increasing the rate of demand, update, decision, replacement, and consequence inside the coupled human system.

It also becomes readable where speed is misread as a property of the self, the market, the machine, or time, while the extracted carrier remains hidden. In that case the acceleration is a form of [[Source Drift]]: the fast process is treated as source, while the slower carrying relation that makes it possible drops out of view.

This distinguishes growth from extraction:

- [[Growth]] names increase where carrying, scale, or available participation increases across traversal.
- Extractive acceleration names apparent increase where faster resolution is purchased by drawing down a slower carrier.
- Collapse begins where resolution-rate exceeds the carrier's ability to renew, repair, or remain compatible.
- Care paces resolution so the carrier remains supportable.

Wisdom, in this diagnosis, is knowing which order is carrying the speed.

This also distinguishes intelligence from extraction. Fast resolution can look intelligent while remaining locally extractive if it depends on hidden drawdown of Earth, bodies, attention, trust, source work, judgement, or repair. Intelligence is not speed of resolution by itself. Intelligence remains answerable to what carries the resolution.

Extraction is speed without retrace.
