# Learning

Learning names participation changing through compatible uptake of what becomes readable.

## Places

Learning places participation changing through compatible uptake of what becomes readable.

## Holds

Learning is held by [[Teaching]], [[Readability]], [[Compatibility]], [[Carrying]], and [[Memory]]. A [[Step]] may make learning answerable in practice, but is not required for learning to occur.

## Pairs

Learning pairs with [[Teaching]]. Teaching carries bearing toward the learner; Learning names what becomes available and carried through that bearing. Teaching and Learning name opposite sides of the same structural juncture.

## Reads

Learning becomes recognisable where a participant can take up a read, correction, practice, step, or condition in a way that changes future carrying without bypassing bearing.

Learning belongs in the teaching domain where uptake must remain traceable through the teaching relation, and the teaching relation must be entered for compatible uptake to become readable rather than merely assumed.

Learning changes the visibility of sequence. Early in learning, the order may need to be stepped through explicitly: notice, act, check, correct, return. As uptake becomes compatible, more of that sequence is carried implicitly through memory, body, attention, or practice. The order does not disappear; it becomes less visible because it is carried more fluidly.

This can change the participant's experience of time. Where every step must be consciously resolved, time may feel granular or slow. Where learned order carries the sequence, time may feel fluid because fewer steps need to return as explicit read.

Learning can tune a participant's resolution band. A sequence that was too fast, too dense, too subtle, too wide, or too unfamiliar to carry can become receivable and integrable through compatible steps. The order is not made real by learning; learning changes how much of the order can be read and carried.
