# Domain

An organised participation context.

Domain is held by [[Field of Participation]] and [[Organic and Organised Organisation]].

A domain also names where general Atlas terms meet a [[Translation Boundary]] and become terminal to local language. The domain does not replace the source structure; it translates that structure into terms the domain can use, test, teach, and repair.

Engineered can be artificial without being unreal. It names a mode of organisation where participation is intentionally, culturally, technically, institutionally, or methodically arranged.

A new domain is justified where participation is intentionally or culturally organised into a recognisable practice, context, tool, discipline, institution, or applied arrangement.

## Places

Domain places an organised participation context. It also places the terminal point where a structural term becomes readable through domain language while remaining retraceable to its Atlas dependency.

## Nests

Domain nests where one or more fields are organised into a context, practice, discipline, tool, institution, or method.

It also nests local terms that are translations of structural Atlas terms at a [[Translation Boundary]].

## Reads

A Domain becomes recognisable where one or more fields are organised into a coherent participation context. A domain is readable where it can show what fields it organises, what branches it carries, and whether its organisation remains retraceable.

It becomes structurally useful where it can show what Atlas term becomes terminal to the domain, what local term carries it from there, and how the path can be retraced.
