# Recursion

Recursion names nested organisation returning into its own carrying.

Where [[Recursive]] names carrying re-entering its own condition at second order, Recursion at higher order names a nested organisation becoming able to participate in the carrying that preserves, revises, or redirects it.

Recurrence, repetition, or circularity can show recursion, but recursion is more specific: organised return into carrying while retrace remains available.

Recursion is powerful because what is carried can become part of the carrying condition. It can preserve, amplify, revise, destabilise, or correct a process once the process begins to act back on itself.

Recursion without retrace can become self-validating drift. Recursion with retrace can become learning.

## Order-Terminal

Recursion is order-terminal for Higher Order. It completes the readable length of nested organisation by letting organisation return into its own carrying. What continues after recursion cannot be read as the same forward passage; it re-enters as a carrying condition that can preserve, revise, amplify, destabilise, or correct itself.
