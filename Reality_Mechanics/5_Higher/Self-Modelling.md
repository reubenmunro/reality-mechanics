# Self-Modelling

Self-Modelling names an engineered form of higher-order carrying where a participant, system, or practice uses a model of itself for future participation.

Self-Modelling is not the organic source of Higher Order. It is an engineered form of recursion.

A model can help retrace where it preserves dependency access. It becomes unsafe where the model becomes the bearing structure and blocks retrace to relation.

## Places

Self-Modelling places an engineered form of higher-order carrying where a participant, system, or practice uses a model of itself for future participation.

## Holds

Self-Modelling is held by [[Recursion]] and [[Consciousness]].

## Pairs

Self-Modelling pairs with [[Self-Regulation]]. Self-Modelling makes self-read available; Self-Regulation uses that read to maintain carrying.

## Traces

- [[Recursion]]
- [[Consciousness]]

## Nests

Self-Modelling nests within higher-order or practice conditions.

## Reads

Self-Modelling becomes recognisable where a participant or system carries a model of itself and uses that model to guide further participation.

It is read by asking whether the model preserves retraceability or begins to substitute for the relation it models.
