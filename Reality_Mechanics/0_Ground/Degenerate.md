# Degenerate

The structural condition where carrying continues while the generative trace is lost — where a term, pattern, or practice carries forward without remaining answerable to the source that generated it.

Degenerate is held by [[Generic]] and [[Structural Disorder]]. A generative source must be operative before it can be lost, and loss of dependency readability must be nameable before its specific form as generative trace loss can be placed.

Degenerate is a specific form of [[Structural Disorder]]: the structure may still function, but it carries as if its source no longer matters. What was generated begins behaving as generator.

This is why [[Practice]] cannot be once-only. Every carried term, pattern, or procedure risks letting its trace become formal rather than live.

## Pairs

Degenerate pairs with [[Regenerate]]. Degenerate names carrying that has lost its generative trace; Regenerate names the return to generative conditions from within that loss. Each requires the other: Degenerate is only nameable against the possibility of returning from it; Regenerate is only nameable against the condition it addresses.

## Traces

- [[Generic]]
- [[Structural Disorder]]

## Nests

Degenerate nests within Structural Disorder as the mode where carrying continues while the generative trace drops.

## Reads

Degenerate becomes recognisable where a term, pattern, or practice carries forward while the trace to its generative origin has been lost — where what was an instance has become a local primitive, and where the form of the source persists without the source itself being held.

The response is [[Regenerate]]: retrace through the carrying, recover the generative source, and carry forward with the origin held rather than dropped.
