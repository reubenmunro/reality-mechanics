# Structural Disorder

Loss of dependency readability in the carrying structure — where what must hold is hidden, interrupted, displaced, bypassed, or forced.

Structural Disorder is held by [[Root Order]].

Structural Disorder is shaded order, not a thing beside order. It names where order cannot be cleanly retraced because what must hold has become obscured, lacking, residue-bearing, or unreadable.

It does not mean evil, failure, immorality, disagreement, difference, or absence of structure. A condition may be active, complex, organised, or stable at a surface level and still be structurally disordered if its dependency path cannot be read.

## Places

Structural Disorder places loss of dependency readability in the carrying structure — the condition where what must hold cannot be read through its dependencies. A condition may be active, complex, organised, or stable at a surface level and still be structurally disordered if its dependency path cannot be traced.

## Holds

Structural Disorder is held by [[Root Order]].

## Pairs

Structural Disorder pairs with [[Groundedness]]. Structural Disorder reveals where order has lost dependency contact; Groundedness names restored or maintained contact.

## Traces

- [[Root Order]]
- [[Dependency Order]]

## Nests

Structural Disorder nests wherever dependency is obscured, displaced, inverted, or treated as optional after it is required.

## Reads

Structural Disorder becomes recognisable where dependency is obscured, displaced, inverted, skipped, or treated as optional after it has become required. It shows disorder in the carrying structure, not merely mess, difference, or instability.
