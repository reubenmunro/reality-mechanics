# Passage Condition

A structural role — terms that regulate whether order can pass, hold, close, or remain available across a boundary or scope.

Passage Condition is held by [[Root Order]]. The governing arrangement must hold before regulative roles within it can be named.

[[Boundary]] and [[Availability]] are the primary passage conditions of the first order. [[Allow]], [[Compatibility]], [[Scope]], and [[Closure Scope]] extend this role into later orders.

## Places

Passage Condition places a structural role within Root Order — the regulative function that determines whether carrying can continue, close locally, be allowed through, or remain available across a boundary or scope.

## Holds

Passage Condition is held by [[Root Order]]. The governing arrangement must hold before regulative roles within it can be named.

## Pairs

Passage Condition pairs with root conditions. Where a root condition lets order emerge upward, a passage condition regulates whether that order can pass or remain available across a scope.

## Traces

- [[Root Order]]
## Nests

Passage Condition nests across orders wherever a boundary or scope must regulate continuation, closure, allowance, or availability.

## Reads

Passage Condition becomes recognisable where a term does not make the next order emerge, but determines whether existing carrying can continue, remain supportable, close locally, be allowed through, or become available inside a given scope.

## Carries
- [[Allow]]
