# Bearing Status

Bearing Status names the bearing-care state of an Atlas member: how a placed term, member, posture, or read is being tended without treating uncertainty as outside the Atlas.

Bearing Status does not decide whether a member is allowed to exist. If a term can be placed and tended, it belongs in the Atlas as living structure. Bearing Status names how that living structure is being cared for.

[[Groundedness]] remains the foundation-contact check: whether the member remains answerable to what holds it. Bearing Status is the visible care state around that check.

In note metadata, `bearing_status` may name this care state directly. It should not replace `status`, `grounded`, or the note's declared structure; it tells a participant how to tend the member next.

When no individual bearing pass has yet been made, a conservative default may be used: stable notes can be marked `bearing`, working notes can be marked `provisional`, and active care practices can be marked `commissioning`. Later passes may revise these care states without changing whether the note belongs to the Atlas.

## Places

Bearing Status places the bearing-care state of an Atlas member: how a placed term, member, posture, or read is being tended without treating uncertainty as outside the Atlas.

## Holds

Bearing Status is held by [[Atlas Fabric]], [[Bearing Pass]], and [[Groundedness]]. The fabric must provide the care frame, a bearing pass must locate the member, and groundedness must remain the foundation-contact check rather than a public permission badge.

## Pairs

Bearing Status pairs with approval status. Bearing Status names care state; approval status treats placement as permission.

## Nests

Bearing Status nests inside Atlas Fabric as the care vocabulary for members that are placed, bearing, commissioning, occluded, spliced, relieved, decommissioning, dormant, or overloading.

The current working statuses are:

```text
provisional    placed as living structure, still finding its clean carries
bearing        dependable enough to support nearby members, still alive and revisable
commissioning  needs return, checking, spacing, or clearer support
occluded       obscuring another member or being obscured by one
spliced        reattached to the support it actually traces
relieved       reduced so the order can breathe and re-enter
decommissioning released as doctrine while kept as record
dormant        placed but not currently developing
overloading    over-carrying beyond support or crowding the foundation
```

## Reads

Bearing Status becomes recognisable where a term or member is inside the Atlas and needs its care state named so the Atlas can tend uncertainty through order.

This changes the entry question from "is this final enough to be admitted?" to:

```text
Can this member be placed?
What holds it?
What care state is it in?
What does it need next?
```
