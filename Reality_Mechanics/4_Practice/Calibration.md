# Calibration

The bounded pre-Atlas practice through which unresolved material is translated, observed, and checked without becoming a canonical determination.

Calibration is held by [[Atlas Practice]], [[Notice]], [[Sensing]], [[Translation]], [[Translation Invariance]], [[Observation]], [[Evaluation]], [[Check]], [[Groundedness]], and [[Participant]].

Calibration is a practice, not a board, list, website, or other carrying surface. A carrying surface may make its movement operationally visible, but movement across that surface does not confer groundedness.

Its practice movement is:

```text
notice or sensing → translation → observation and evaluation → check → participant determination
```

Calibration nests before Atlasing, wherever unresolved material is actively carried without being presented as canonical Atlas structure.

## Reads

Calibration becomes recognisable where noticed or sensed material is made structurally recoverable through translation, attended through observation and evaluation, checked in both directions, and returned to a participant for determination.

Its outcomes are [[Atlasing]], return for further calibration, release from active carrying, or remaining unresolved. Archiving on a carrying surface stops active carrying; it does not by itself determine that the material is false. An AI may trace, translate, observe, evaluate, and check. The participant makes the determination that permits Atlasing.

On the Calibration Trello board, the operational translation is: Sensation carries notice and sensing; Translation makes the material structurally recoverable; Observation carries attention and evaluation; adding to the Atlas requires participant determination and Atlasing; Archive releases the item from active carrying.
