# Decommissioning

Decommissioning names the structural practice of releasing failed or relieved material as doctrine while keeping what it taught available to the order.

Decommissioning is not deletion. It is the practice of letting a claim stop carrying authority without erasing the trace of its failure. A failed posture can show what the order could not hold, what became occluded, where re-entry collapsed, or which dependency was missing.

## Places

Decommissioning places the structural practice of releasing failed or relieved material as doctrine while keeping what it taught available to the order.

## Holds

Decommissioning is held by [[Bearing Pass]], [[Release]], and [[Root Resilience]]. A bearing pass must identify the material, release must let the claim go, and root resilience must keep the failure from corrupting the dependency order.

## Pairs

No lateral pair is required. Decommissioning is a care action inside Bearing Pass.

## Traces

- [[Bearing Pass]]
- [[Release]]
- [[Root Resilience]]

## Nests

Decommissioning nests inside Atlas Fabric wherever failed postures, released claims, or relieved member material can become record without remaining doctrine.

## Reads

Decommissioning becomes recognisable where failed material is released from carrying authority but retained as traceable record about what could not hold, carry, place, clear, or clean.

The decommissioning question is:

```text
What claim must stop carrying?
What did the failure reveal?
What trace should remain so the failure can teach?
What needs to be released so the foundation can stay clean?
```
