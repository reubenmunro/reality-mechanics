# Atlas Note Standard

Atlas Note Standard names the placement standard by which an interpretation becomes a term inside the [[Atlas]].

An Atlas note is a mapped reasoning position, not a glossary entry. Its form exists to make order explicit: what the term places, what holds it, what it pairs with, where it traces, where it nests, how it reads, and what it carries.

In the garden read, an Atlas note is also a planted growth. Its note form places it; its [[Garden Status]] names how it is being tended. A working note is not outside the Atlas. It is inside the Atlas as living structure whose care state remains visible.

The `garden_status` field may name that care state in metadata. It is not a permission field; it is a tending signal.

## Places

Atlas Note Standard places the note spine:

```text
Places
Holds
Pairs
Traces
Nests
Reads
Carries
```

These are term conditions. They let a note be mapped, compared, corrected, grounded, and retraced.

## Holds

Atlas Note Standard is held by the term conditions. Holds names the nearest condition doing the bearing.

## Pairs

Pairs names what the term must be read with, against, or beside in order to remain locatable. A pair is the minimum carrying relation, not only an opposite.

Not every term requires a lateral pair. Root, order, invariant, class-forming, vertical, or terminal terms may carry downward instead.

## Nests

Nests names the closure level where the term holds and the larger or smaller reads it can sit within without losing trace.

## Reads

Atlas Note Standard becomes recognisable where a note can be placed, compared, revised, grounded, and retraced because its term conditions remain explicit.

The note should state the term directly, show its immediate support, name how it becomes recognisable, and show what it carries forward. Explanation serves placement.

Garden status does not replace the note spine. It protects the note spine from becoming a permission system. If a term can be placed and tended, it can enter as a planted growth while its holds, traces, shade, and future care remain answerable.

The two dependency directions are not interchangeable:

```text
generative direction: prior term ──carries──▶ downstream term
evidential direction: prior term ◀──traces── downstream term
```

Reality may carry a condition before that carrying is read. The Atlas, however, grounds a `Carries` claim only where a downstream term has become available and its `Traces` can recover the dependency. What a term may make possible remains an opening until a return trace confirms the continuation. Prediction does not by itself establish carrying.

A downstream term may trace several prior conditions and the passage through which they participate. The test does not force a single source where the dependency is multiple. Failure of an anticipated downstream term also does not by itself disprove bearing from an upstream condition; allowance, compatibility, boundary, interruption, or other passage conditions may have prevented continuation.

## Carries


- [[Check]]
- [[Atlasing]]
- [[Terms and Conditions]]
- [[Carry-Trace Test]]
- [[Discipline]]

Carries names confirmed continuation from the term into downstream terms. Possible continuation belongs to available openings until it becomes recoverable through a downstream trace.

Carries and Traces record dependency. If a note carries a term, that term traces back to it. Nested placement belongs in Nests; dependency belongs in Traces and Carries.
