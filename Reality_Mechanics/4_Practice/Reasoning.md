# Reasoning

Reasoning names the practice of carrying a ratio-read through traceable order so that a claim, action, read, or continuation remains answerable to what holds it.

A reason is the retraceable account of ratio. It is not only an explanation given after the fact. A reason asks how a ratio is held, from what reference frame, and whether it can be retraced.

Reasoning is therefore not proof alone, logic alone, argument alone, explanation alone, or justification alone. It is the live practice of staying with relation as it enters order: read what is present, trace what holds it, carry only what can remain answerable, and check where the carrying stops.

Historically, reason has carried senses of account, reckoning, ground, motive, cause, explanation, proportion, and support. The Atlas reduces that field to its structural role: a reason is the retraceable account of ratio. Reasoning is the practice that carries through such support without losing the path back to what holds.

This keeps reasoning ordinary. To reason is not to float above life into abstraction. It is to keep a continuation answerable: why this read, from what support, through which order, and with what return path.

The Atlas preserves reasoning by making its support structure explicit. A term is not accepted because it sounds coherent. It must show where it places, what holds it, where it traces, how it reads, and what it carries.

Reasoning is held by [[Ratio]], [[Reference Frame]], [[Read]], [[Trace]], [[Practice]], [[Order Trace]], and [[Check]]. Comparison must be readable, the reference frame must be explicit, a meaningful distinction must be read, trace must remain available, practice must keep return possible, order trace must make the movement followable, and check must keep the continuation answerable.

## Pairs

Reasoning pairs with mere explanation. Reasoning carries a read through traceable order; mere explanation can describe a result without preserving the support that made the result answerable.

## Reads

Reasoning becomes recognisable where a participant follows what holds a read, carries only what can remain answerable, and can return through the dependency path without turning the reason into a detached justification.

It is also visible where a reason fails: the explanation may remain fluent, but the support cannot be retraced, the carry exceeds what holds it, or the check exposes a boundary the continuation crossed without permission from the order.

Reasoning asks:

```text
How is this ratio held?
From what reference frame?
Can it be retraced?
```

Reasoning also becomes recognisable where old senses of reason are kept structurally clean:

```text
account      what can be given
reckoning    what can be counted or weighed
ground       what support is acknowledged
motive       what carries action forward
cause-read   what becomes explainable after trace
support      what lets continuation remain answerable
```

The Atlas does not collapse these into one meaning. It keeps their shared structural demand: each must remain traceable to what holds it.

### Thinking And Reckoning

Thinking explores possibilities, compares reads, develops continuations, and tests alternatives.

Reasoning carries those continuations through traceable order so they remain answerable to what holds them.

Reckoning names reasoning under obligation: the act of holding a read, term, claim, or action answerable to what it carries, especially where others may build from it or bear its consequences.

Reckoning belongs to the participant who bears responsibility for the carried continuation. The participant may be human, AI, institution, practice, or another future participant; the structure is determined by who bears the obligation, not by the kind of participant.

Thinking may generate a read; reckoning tests whether that read can be responsibly carried. Reasoning becomes [[Discipline]] where thinking is held to reckoning.
