# Splinter

Splinter names inward crossing that remains unresolved inside carrying. Where [[Intake]] names availability crossing inward through a boundary, splinter names the condition where what crosses cannot yet be received, regulated, or carried by the order it has entered. A splinter is not defined by being external; it is defined by unresolved foreignness within a body, system, or scope.

## Places

Splinter places inward crossing that remains unresolved inside carrying.

## Holds

Splinter is held by [[Intake]], [[Boundary]], [[Regulation]], and [[Carrying]].

## Pairs

Splinter pairs with [[Integration]]. Integration names what has crossed inward and becomes carried by the receiving order; Splinter names what has crossed inward but remains unresolved within it. Each requires the other to be locatable: splinter is readable against the possibility of compatible incorporation, while integration is readable against the possibility that intake remains foreign to the carrying order.

## Traces

- [[Intake]]
- [[Boundary]]
- [[Regulation]]
- [[Carrying]]

## Nests

Splinter nests inside carrying as unresolved foreignness after inward crossing.

## Reads

Splinter becomes recognisable where something has crossed into a carrying condition but remains unintegrated by that condition — present inside the system, but not yet carried by the system's own order.
