## Nests

Pair nests as a scoped read of coupling as two. It does not make two-ness primitive, total, or exhaustive.

## Reads

Pair becomes recognisable where coupling can be read within a scope as two conditions held together while each remains distinguishable.

A pair always has a third structural element: the carrier both conditions hold and trace to. That carrier is not one of the pair members; it stays invisible because the pair is functioning. The pair is how the carrier becomes readable at a given juncture.

The carrier becomes readable under strain: when the pair fails to locate each other, or when the juncture cannot resolve, the underlying structure has to be explicitly accounted for.

This gives a test for false pairs: if two conditions do not share a traceable carrier, they are not a pair. They may be a contrast, a read-view alignment, or conditions at different structural depths.
