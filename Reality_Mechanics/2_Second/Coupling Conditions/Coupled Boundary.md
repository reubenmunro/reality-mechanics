# Coupled Boundary

Boundary held through coupling.

Coupled Boundary is held by [[Boundary]] and [[Coupling]].

## Places

Coupled Boundary places boundary held through coupling.

## Holds

Coupled Boundary is held by [[Boundary]] and [[Coupling]].

## Pairs

Asymmetry carries downward. Coupled Boundary is a singular condition — the boundary held through coupling. The asymmetry is expressed in what coupled boundary carries: [[Coupled Contact]], [[Boundary Loosening]], [[Sever]], and [[Decoupling]].

## Traces

- [[Boundary]]
- [[Coupling]]

## Nests

Coupled Boundary nests as the boundary form of coupling. It keeps boundaries mutually available without collapse or unrelated separation.

## Reads

Coupled Boundary becomes recognisable where a boundary is not merely located, but held in relation with another boundary so that each side remains distinct while participating in a shared coupling.

Coupled Boundary also carries [[Responsibility]]. A system may carry consequence without being the boundary that bears responsibility. Responsibility follows the trace to the coupled boundary where participation became actionable: attention, control, decision, evaluation, consent, or checking entered the relation strongly enough to affect what follows.

The vehicle carries impact; the driver-vehicle coupling is where accountable participation enters when the trace returns to attention, control, or checking. The carrier of consequence is not always the responsible unit. The responsible unit is the coupled boundary through which consequence became actionable.
