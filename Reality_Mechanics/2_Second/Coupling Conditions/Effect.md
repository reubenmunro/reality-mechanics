# Effect

Change in one condition carried through coupling from another condition.

Effect is held by [[Coupling]], [[Carrying]], and [[Change]]. Conditions must be coupled, carrying must pass through the relation, and a readable change must occur before effect can be named.

Change alone does not establish effect. Two conditions may change together without one change being carried through their relation. Effect becomes available where the change remains traceable through coupling: what one condition carries alters what the other can hold, do, present, or continue.

## Places

Effect places change in one condition carried through coupling from another condition.

## Holds

Effect is held by [[Coupling]], [[Carrying]], and [[Change]]. Conditions must be coupled, carrying must pass through the relation, and a readable change must occur before effect can be named.

## Pairs

Effect is vertical. It is the singular carried change from which participatory alteration can later be read as [[Influence]]. No lateral opposite is required: absence of effect may indicate no carrying, incompatible coupling, insufficient resolution, or a change outside the current scope.

## Nests

Effect nests inside coupling where carrying from one condition becomes readable as change in another, whether contact is direct or mediated.

## Reads

Effect becomes recognisable where a change can be traced through a coupled relation to carrying from another condition rather than only occurring alongside it.

Effect does not by itself name intention, benefit, harm, control, or responsibility. Those are later reads of what the carried change does within participation.
