# Traction

Traction names grip becoming available for carrying through coupled contact. It is the condition through which carrying can proceed without slipping away from the support it needs. Connection and resistance can support traction, but traction names enough resistance to let carrying hold.

## Places

Traction places grip becoming available for carrying through coupled contact.

## Holds

Traction is held by [[Grip]], [[Coupled Contact]], and [[Carrying]].

## Pairs

Asymmetry carries downward. Traction is a singular condition — grip made into carrying through coupled contact. There is no lateral contrast required at this level.

## Traces

- [[Grip]]
- [[Coupled Contact]]
- [[Contact]]
- [[Carrying]]
- [[Friction]]

## Nests

Traction nests in carrying through coupled contact as the usable middle between slipping and stalling.

## Reads

Traction becomes recognisable where coupled contact and grip support carrying through resistance without detaching from support.
