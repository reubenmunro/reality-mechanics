# Coupling

Connected relation — distinct resolved conditions held in mutual availability without collapsing into sameness.

Coupled relation connects. Contact can meet without coupling; a link can point without bearing; a category can group without participation. Coupling holds distinct resolved conditions in mutual availability so that relation can become locally present, answerable, and able to participate.

## Places

Coupling places connected relation — distinct resolved conditions held in mutual availability without collapsing into sameness.

## Holds

Coupling is held by [[Resolved Asymmetry]] and [[Carrying]]. Distinct conditions must be determined and carried enough to become mutually available.

## Pairs

Coupling pairs with [[Decoupling]]: coupling holds distinct resolved conditions in mutual availability; decoupling names that mutual availability no longer holding.

## Traces

- [[Resolved Asymmetry]]
- [[Carrying]]
- [[Union]]

## Nests

Coupling nests as a second-order support root. It makes presence, compatibility, identity, coupled contact, pair, and bearing relation possible without collapsing coupled conditions into sameness.

## Reads

Coupling becomes recognisable where two distinct resolved conditions can be read as connected relation — held in relation to each other, mutually available, and still distinct.
