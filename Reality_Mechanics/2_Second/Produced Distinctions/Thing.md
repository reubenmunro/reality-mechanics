# Thing

A scale-local stability of relation.

Thing is held by [[Stability]], [[Scale]], [[Relative]], [[Closure Scope]], [[Structure]], and [[Readability]]. Relation must hold together as structure, remain stable enough at a scale, be placed through relation, close locally enough for evaluation, and become readable before it can be named or carried as a thing.

A thing-read can drift where a relation compressed into a noun is treated as if the noun must contain the whole referent. The dropped conditions were dropped from the description, not from reality. The repair is not to hunt for a hidden object inside the noun, but to reopen the relation, scale, and closure scope that made the thing readable.

## Reads

Thing becomes readable where a relational stability can be named, used, carried, or depended on at the scale and scope where it holds.

This preserves usefulness without isolating the object. A beam is still a beam at the building scale and within the closure scope of building action. It is also fibres, bonds, grain, stress, moisture, fixings, and load path at other scales and scopes. The thing-read holds where the scale-local stability is real enough to carry participation.

The identity of a thing is therefore relative in the structural sense: not arbitrary, but placed through the relations that compose it, the relations it participates in, and the closure scope where it is being read.

When relation is nominalized, this carrying can become hidden. Re-entering the thing-read means asking what relation, scale, and closure scope the name has compressed. [[Relative]] keeps the object-read answerable to the relation and scope that placed it.
