# Member

Member names a scale-local participating part in a carried body.

A member is not an absolute identity. It is the placed participation a thing has where it belongs to a wider structure, field, body, system, or relation. A beam is a member at the frame scale. A person is a member at the family, team, community, or civic scale. At another scale, each is also made of smaller members and participates in larger ones.

Member preserves the thing-read without isolating it. A member carries itself, receives load or relation, and resolves what it carries into a wider body.

## Places

Member places a scale-local participating part in a carried body.

## Holds

Member is held by [[Thing]], [[Scale]], [[Structure]], [[Carrying]], and [[Participation]]. A thing must be readable at a scale, ordered enough to bear, and participating in a wider carried body before it can be read as a member.

## Pairs

No lateral pair is required at this placement yet. Unit is a useful contrast: Unit names a bounded repeatable measure; Member names a scale-local participating part in a carried body.

A unit can be counted or compared without belonging to a body. A member is readable through belonging, bearing, and participation.

## Nests

Member nests where a thing is made of smaller members, carries itself, receives load or relation, and resolves what it carries into a wider body.

At each scale, the member-read changes without breaking the relation. A structural member may be a beam in a frame, timber fibres in a beam, or a frame within a building. A human member may be a person in a family, organs in a body, cells in an organ, or a community within a wider field.

## Reads

Member becomes readable where a thing is located by its participation in a wider carried body rather than by isolated identity.

A [[Label]] may mark the current member-read for communication, coordination, or action. Beam, stud, rafter, post, citizen, patient, parent, worker, tool, and obstacle can each name a placed read. The member-read remains answerable to placement, scale, carrying, and participation.

This makes member a scale term as much as a belonging term. It names a recurring operation: carrying self-weight, receiving relation, and resolving what is carried into support, continuity, or participation.
