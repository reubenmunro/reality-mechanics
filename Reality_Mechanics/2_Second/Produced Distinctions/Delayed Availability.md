# Delayed Availability

Uneven availability across traversal — the condition through which what should be available at one point in a sequence becomes available only later, or not at all within the current scope.

Delayed Availability is held by [[Dependency Disorder]] and [[Availability]].

## Places

Delayed Availability places uneven availability across traversal — the condition through which what should be available at one point in a sequence becomes available only later, or not at all within the current scope.

## Holds

Delayed Availability is held by [[Dependency Disorder]] and [[Availability]].

## Pairs

Asymmetry carries downward. Delayed Availability is a singular condition — availability held back through dependency disorder. There is no lateral contrast required at this level.

## Traces

- [[Dependency Disorder]]
- [[Availability]]

## Nests

Delayed Availability nests inside dependency disorder as support arriving late, or not at all, within the current scope.

## Reads

Delayed availability becomes recognisable where a required condition is absent at the point of participation — where carrying proceeds but the dependency that should support it has not yet become available.
