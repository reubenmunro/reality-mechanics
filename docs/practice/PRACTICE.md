# Reality Mechanics Practice

## Purpose

The purpose of the Reality Mechanics Practice is to reduce uncertainty until a steward can make a grounded decision.

The practice does not exist to produce software, documents, reports, websites, or releases. Those are artefacts.

Its purpose is to establish enough evidence that the next decision becomes retraceable.

---

## Core Flow

Every commission begins with pressure.

Every contract defines a question.

Every investigation gathers evidence.

Every recommendation follows from evidence.

Every steward decision either resolves or continues the pressure.

Reality has the final authority.

```text
Pressure
↓
Commission
↓
Contract
↓
Evidence
↓
Recommendation
↓
Decision
↓
Resolution
```

---

## Pressure

Pressure is the condition that makes work necessary.

A pressure may be an uncertainty, conflict, instability, missing relation, failed test, unresolved dependency, or release risk.

Pressure is not yet a task.

Pressure becomes actionable only when the steward commissions a response.

---

## Commission

A commission is the steward's act of authorising attention.

It does not prescribe the answer.

It names the pressure and authorises the architect to prepare a bounded response.

A good commission makes clear what decision is being sought.

---

## Contract

A contract converts a commission into a bounded question.

It states:

- the governing question
- the scope
- what is out of scope
- the evidence required
- the deliverable
- the acceptance standard

A contract is not an informal task. It is a bounded carrying structure for pressure.

---

## Evidence

Evidence is what the contractor returns.

Evidence may include repository facts, command outputs, test results, citations, reports, failed attempts, missing files, unresolved assumptions, or countable relations.

Evidence is not recommendation.

Evidence says what is.

---

## Recommendation

A recommendation is the architect's reading of the evidence.

It should explain whether the evidence satisfies the contract, what relation has changed, what remains unresolved, and what decision is now possible.

A recommendation must not exceed the evidence.

---

## Decision

A decision belongs to the steward.

The architect may recommend.

The contractor may report.

The repository may preserve.

Only the steward accepts, rejects, or continues a commission.

---

## Resolution

Resolution is the state reached when the pressure has been sufficiently carried for a grounded decision to stand.

Resolution does not always mean completion.

Sometimes resolution means:

- accepted
- rejected
- blocked
- deferred
- continued
- proven unresolved

A pressure is resolved when its current relation is known enough for the steward to act.

---

## Standard of Care

The practice should prefer:

- evidence over assertion
- relation over percentage
- bounded contracts over informal tasks
- repository truth over memory
- explicit unknowns over invented certainty
- steward decision over AI momentum

---

## Separation from Atlas and Stewardship

This document describes the practice by which work is commissioned, carried, reviewed, and resolved.

It does not define Atlas content.

It does not replace the Stewardship specification.

It does not define platform architecture.

It sits beside those bodies as practice governance.
