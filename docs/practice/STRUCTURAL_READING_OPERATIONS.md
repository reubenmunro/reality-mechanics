# Structural Reading Operations

**Practice artefact.** Operations extracted from evidence — not promoted Calculus, not a style guide.

This document records **repeatable structural operations** observed when Atlas opening prose becomes more faithful to structure through Structural Reading.

**Evidence base:** D-006 (Connection); D-010 (Hold, Clear, Shift, Release, Tact).

Operations appear here only when they recur across **multiple independent refinements**. Single-instance patterns remain unknown.

No promotion. Steward decides whether an operation becomes load-bearing discipline.

---

## Operation — hold before movement

**Description**

State what holds the place — the term's `needs` / `holds` — **before** naming what the place carries, opens, or makes possible.

**Observed examples**

| Term | Before | After (operation) |
|---|---|---|
| Connection (D-006) | Carry/Trace before Relation hold | Relation hold sentence precedes directional reads |
| Hold | [[Carry]] named before [[Resolution]] hold in opening | Resolution hold precedes Carry |
| Clear | [[Clearance]] opened before Clean/Relation hold | Clean and Relation hold precede Clearance |
| Shift | [[Hold]]/[[Yield]] pair read before Resolution/Orientation hold | Resolution and Orientation hold precede pair comparison |
| Release | Second-order Retain read before Resolution/Bearing/Strain hold | Multi-need hold precedes scale paragraph |
| Tact | [[Contact]] opened before Clean/Relation hold | Clean and Relation hold precede Contact |

**Evidence**

Six independent refinements (five D-010 terms + D-006). In every case, automated D-008 read flagged **inverted dependency order** in opening prose; frontmatter already named the hold in `## Holds`.

**Confidence**

**High** — recurs on every Tier 1 calibration and on the prior D-006 control case.

**Failure mode if omitted**

Reader encounters movement or carried terms without retraced ancestry; language becomes interpretive; Place/Movement read shifts to **movement obscures place**.

## Operation — places field alignment

**Description**

Extend or align the opening place sentence with `conditions.places` so the place is named completely in one retraceable sentence before hold or movement.

**Observed examples**

| Term | Operation |
|---|---|
| Connection (D-006) | Added "so that passage is available in more than one direction" from `places` |
| Clear | Added em-dash clause from `places`: "where relation is unobstructed enough for clearance to be held" |
| Tact | Added em-dash clause from `places`: handling side with contact constraint |
| Shift | Added "transferring" from `places` to opening negative read |
| Hold | Opening already matched `places`; no extension required |

**Evidence**

Four alignments across six calibrations (Hold already aligned; Release place sentence already complete).

**Confidence**

**Medium-high** — strong recurrence; Hold shows the operation is **conditional** (apply when opening under-names the place).

**Failure mode if omitted**

Opening duplicates or under-specifies `## Places`; reader must infer the place from template sections.

## Operation — carried availability after hold

**Description**

After the hold sentence, name carried terms as **availability offered from a held place** — not as "opens X" before the hold is stated.

**Observed examples**

| Term | Operation |
|---|---|
| Connection (D-006) | "Held connection offers [[Carry]] forward and [[Trace]] backward" after Relation hold |
| Hold | "Hold makes [[Carry]] possible" after Resolution hold |
| Clear | "Held clear offers [[Clearance]]…" after Clean/Relation hold |
| Tact | "Held tact offers [[Contact]]" after Clean/Relation hold |

**Evidence**

Four independent refinements. Shift and Release name place and hold without listing all carries in opening — carries remain in template sections.

**Confidence**

**Medium** — recurs where opening prose named a single carried term early; not required when opening stops at place + hold.

**Failure mode if omitted**

"Opens X" language implies movement before the hold that bears it; retrace fails at the opening paragraph.

## Unknown — wikilink restoration

**Description (provisional)**

Where prose names an Atlas term without a wikilink, add the link so the name retraces.

**Observed examples**

| Term | Instance |
|---|---|
| Hold | `bearing` / `resolution` → [[Bearing]] / [[Resolution]] |
| Clear | `clean` → [[Clean]] in distinction sentence |

**Evidence**

Two instances only (D-010). D-006 used removal and hold-before-movement rather than wikilink restoration.

**Confidence**

**Low** — insufficient for promotion. Remains under observation.

**Failure mode if omitted**

Prose names look like ordinary language; dependency set unclear in opening.

**Status:** Unknown — needs more independent occurrences before operation status.

## Unknown — pair comparison deferred

**Description (provisional)**

Move lateral `pairs` comparison (e.g. Hold vs Yield) to **after** the hold sentence.

**Observed examples**

| Term | Instance |
|---|---|
| Shift | Hold/Yield comparison moved after Resolution/Orientation hold |

**Evidence**

One instance only.

**Confidence**

**Low** — may be a special case of **hold before movement**.

**Status:** Unknown — not promoted separately.

## Relation to Structural Reading

These operations are **Pass 5 instruments** — discovered from Pass 1–4 reads, not invented before evidence.

They do not replace the five-pass discipline in `docs/practice/STRUCTURAL_READING.md`.

When an operation cannot be justified for a term, **do not force the edit** (D-006 / D-010 rule).
