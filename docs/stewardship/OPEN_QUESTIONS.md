# Open Questions

Only genuinely unresolved matters. Solved questions belong in `AUDIT_LOG.md`, not here. Three categories, kept strictly separate — do not blur them:

- **Confirmed Atlas Gaps** — investigated directly and concluded to be real, but not filled.
- **Pressure** — a repeated pattern worth continued attention, not yet promoted to method or invariant, and not requiring resolution.
- **Future Investigations** — a specific question with insufficient evidence in either direction.

---

## Confirmed Atlas Gaps

### Second Order has no order-level terminal marker

Unlike First Order (Resolution), Third Order (Nesting), and Higher Order (Recursion), Second Order has no entry carrying `is_terminal: true`. This was investigated directly and specifically — not merely unsearched — across multiple families before reaching a conclusion.

**Why this is treated as a genuine asymmetry, not an omission:**
1. The property itself is already non-uniform where it exists: optional at the First-order crossing boundary (Resolution is explicitly not required for every instance of carrying), mandatory at the Third-order crossing boundary (Nesting is a required, formally-cited dependency of Third Order Crossing).
2. Second Order's own audited material, across two separate family audits (Family 3 and Family 2), is consistently accumulative — continuation, participation, recurrence — never determinative in the way Strain → Bearing → Resolution is.
3. The one plausible internal candidate, Coupling, was directly tested and found to answer a structurally different question (a foundation/joining question — "how do two distinct things become mutually available without collapsing" — not an exhaustion question about an order's own material running out).

**Instruction to future stewards: do not invent a Second Order terminal marker.** If this is revisited, it requires the same burden of proof as any other structural claim — new evidence, not restored symmetry for its own sake.

## Pressure

### The "collapse-preventing dependency" pattern

In at least two compound terms (Recarry's Compatibility; Contact's Tact), one dependency's specific job is preventing the mechanic from collapsing into a worse, adjacent failure-mode (Collapse; interference/collision/harm) rather than contributing to the mechanic's basic occurrence. Two independent instances is a real, repeated shape — not yet enough to promote to a named test or invariant. Watch for a third instance before considering promotion.

### Unresolved residue in every tested compound's sufficiency analysis

In Resolution (Bearing), Coupling (Resolved Asymmetry), and Recarry (Carrying), exactly one dependency fails both halves of the Generation Sufficiency Test — its removal produces plain absence, not a rival named mechanic and not a disconnected-but-intact mechanic. This recurred three times across three separate compounds and was deliberately not promoted to a third category, per the discipline of not inventing vocabulary the Atlas hasn't repeatedly forced beyond what's already been tested. If a fourth compound shows the identical pattern, that is the point to revisit — not before.

### Crossing-architecture non-uniformity across order boundaries

First Order treats its terminal marker as optional for crossing; Third Order treats its terminal marker as mandatory; Second Order has none. This is confirmed and non-defective (see Confirmed Atlas Gaps, above) but the full characterization of *why* orders differ this way — is it about the fundamental character of each order's mechanic, or something else — is not complete.

## Future Investigations

### Interposed Carrier's "carrying" language

Its Places, Reads, and own name repeatedly invoke "carrying," but neither of its two dependencies (Contact, Boundary) supplies a carrying-quality, and no independent second source has been checked to corroborate or refute a missing-dependency reading. This sits genuinely between a confirmed gap (like Release) and a safe casual mention (like Yield's "strain") — the evidence doesn't clearly favor either conclusion.

**Instruction:** if revisited, test independently. Do not resolve by resemblance to Release, Field of Participation, Engineered Coupling, Nested Coupling, or Maintained Coupling — each of those was confirmed on its own specific evidence, and this case has not yet met that bar in either direction.

### Whether the Places→Term "carving" pattern recurs beyond Carry/Trace

Confirmed once, cleanly: Carry and Trace are one underlying condition (held Connection + Hold availability) carved into two terms by direction. One instance is a hypothesis, not a pattern. Worth checking against other paired-direction terms if any surface in future audits.

### Whether Second Order's accumulative character is unique to it or a matter of degree

Related to the confirmed gap above, but a separate, unresolved question in its own right: is "accumulative rather than determinative" a binary property some orders have and others don't, or a spectrum every order sits somewhere on? Not yet characterized.
