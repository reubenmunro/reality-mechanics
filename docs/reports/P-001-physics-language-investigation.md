# P-001 — Physics as the Language of Reality

**Programme:** Research  
**Type:** Read-only investigation (no implementation, no Observatory redesign)  
**Date:** 2026-07-06 (UTC+12)  
**Governing constraint:** Reality Mechanics is **not** assumed to be physics. Physics is humanity's most mature language for making observable behaviour visible. This report asks whether it is the **natural expressive language** for structures already present in the Atlas and runtime.

**Evidence read:** `MISSION.md`, `docs/CONSTITUTION.md`, Atlas (`Relation`, `Ratio`, `Availability`, `Carry`, `Trace`, `Pressure`, third-order field reads), `Theory.md`, `public-surface-manifest.mjs` (Calculus derivation chain), `docs/runtime/DERIVED_RATIO.md`, `docs/practice/RUNTIME_PRINCIPLES.md`, `docs/reports/D-011`, `D-012`, `D-014`, `D-015B`, `C-A001`, `O-001`, `.atlas-publisher/field-behaviour-trace.mjs`, `.atlas-publisher/main-website-worker.js` (renderer behaviour, not redesign).

---

## Verdict

**B — Physics is only partially appropriate.**

Reality Mechanics, read on its own terms, naturally produces **field-adjacent** structure: continuous readability media, local emergence under load, directional propagation along held connection, and stability reads where distinction resolves. These resemble **fields, gradients, trajectories, condensation, and equilibrium-like stability** — but as **relational and readability phenomena**, not as a physical ontology.

Physics is an **excellent rendering language** for the **scalar and phenomenological ratios the Observatory already computes**. It is **not** a faithful ontology for Atlas structure. Where Atlas and physics disagree, the Atlas disagreement is load-bearing and must be preserved.

Negative results are part of the finding: **conservation laws, potentials, permeability from availability, and unified field equations do not currently derive** from repository evidence.

## 1. Research summary

### 1.1 What Reality Mechanics is trying to do

`MISSION.md` states the purpose plainly: **increase structural perception** — not invent structure. The working postulate (`Theory.md`) tests whether relation, order, and trace are minimum conditions for valid Atlas placement:

```text
Relation holds.
Order carries.
Trace places.
```

The Constitution preserves **retraceability** and **evidence over memory**. The Atlas is a dependency-ordered reasoning system; terms are **placed operations**, not substances. `Relation` is **primitive**; `Ratio` is relation **made readable** between distinguishable terms — "not first a number" (`Ratio.md`).

### 1.2 What the runtime already does

The Observatory translates canonical structure into observable behaviour through a **narrow scalar chain** (D-015B: viable with constraints):

```text
holds/traces in-degree → mass.carriers → ratioMode → client expansion → ratio-modulated render
```

Parallel paths also read structure **directly** (D-015B falsification): edge existence, layout bands, reference-frame visibility. Render compression is **readability under budget**, not recognition (D-014 rejection).

The renderer additionally uses physics-**named** quantities (`enginePhysics`: gravity, velocity, friction, pressure, damping; `pressureField` grid; `fieldTensionFromGradient`; condensation; trajectories). Most of these are **calibrated or heuristic render translations**, not Atlas-derived field equations.

### 1.3 Central question — isolation thought experiment

If Reality Mechanics were developed in complete isolation from modern physics, its derivations would likely still produce:

| Structure | Likely without physics textbooks |
|---|---|
| Held connection with forward/backward availability | **Yes** — `Carry` / `Trace` are carved from one condition by direction (`STEWARDSHIP_V1.md`; `Carry.md`, `Trace.md`) |
| Readable comparison between distinguishable terms | **Yes** — `Ratio` as clearance-bounded comparison, not quantity first |
| Local emergence where load meets relation | **Yes** — Atlas `Pressure` as "emergent load becoming locally borne through relation" |
| Pattern reads for how availability changes | **Yes** — third-order `Field Availability Pattern`, `Physical Field Conditions` |
| Scalar carrier density affecting readability | **Yes** — runtime `mass.carriers` emerged from structure scan (D-010B, D-011) |
| Continuous visual medium for invisible structure | **Plausible** — Observatory mission implies an instrument; O-001 moved home view toward membrane-first for this reason |

They would **unlikely** produce, without borrowing physics:

| Physics structure | Why unlikely from current Atlas alone |
|---|---|
| Conservation laws | No Atlas term or runtime invariant states quantity conservation |
| Lagrangian / Hamiltonian formalism | No action principle; no state space over configurations |
| Potentials whose gradients uniquely determine motion | No derived potential field; renderer pressure is deposited scalar, not ∇φ |
| Metric curvature as geometry of relation | Curvature in renderer is visual bow (`fieldBend`), not derived geodesic structure |
| Particles with continuous field interaction | Coupling is **declared-edge-only** (D-011 O-4) |

**Conclusion:** Isolated development would likely arrive at a **relational field phenomenology** — readability, load, availability, propagation along connection — and might **independently invent field-like visualization** to make that legible. It would not automatically arrive at **physics as theory of reality**.

## 7. Implications for the Observatory

### 7.1 Should it evolve from graph renderer to field renderer?

**Yes — conceptually, partially, and only as instrument architecture.**

Evidence supports the O-001 direction:

| Graph renderer habit | Field renderer habit | Grounding |
|---|---|---|
| Terms as primary dots | Medium visible first; terms condense at stability | `structuralMass`, condensation (heuristic) |
| Edges as straight/decorated lines | Currents through deposited pressure | `pressureGradientAt`, declared edges |
| Labels on placement | Labels on interaction (probe) | D-023 hover probe |
| Network diagram framing | Instrument framing | Mission: structural perception |

**Limits (must not be crossed):**

- Do not treat the membrane as ontology (O-001 explicit).
- Do not draw undeclared edges for visual continuity (P1, D-011 O-4).
- Do not claim render pressure is Atlas Pressure until derived (C-A001).
- Do not introduce equations without ratios (O-001 acceptance standard).
- Focused mode remains layout-band / graph-adjacent until unified (O-001 §4).

### 7.2 Conceptual architecture shift (not implementation)

```text
Today (mixed):
  structure → layout graph → decorate with physics metaphors

Target direction:
  structure → scalar reads → continuous medium → legible stabilisations → interaction reveals terms
```

The Observatory should remain a **field instrument** in the sense of **scientific instrumentation**: making invisible structure perceptible through a disciplined translation layer — not simulating a physical world.

Physics vocabulary is appropriate where it **maps to existing ratios**:

- mass-like → `structuralMass` / carriers  
- tension-like → hold rhythm / gradient tension  
- trajectory-like → declared relation paths biased by local pressure  
- stability-like → settledness / condensation thresholds  

Physics vocabulary is inappropriate where it **implies ontology**:

- forces acting without declared relation  
- conservation without invariant  
- potentials without derivation  
- availability as permeability without ratio

## 10. Steward summary

Physics is the **best available rendering language** for the Observatory's **existing scalar reads and phenomenological behaviours** — pressure deposition, gradient bias, condensation at stability, propagation along declared connection. It is **not** the natural **foundational mathematics** of Reality Mechanics, which remains **relational, directional, and retraceable** before it is continuous.

The honest posture for the programme:

> Use physics to make ratios legible. Do not use physics to claim what the Atlas has not derived.

O-001 was consistent with this verdict: field-first rendering grounded in available ratios, heuristic parts labelled, gaps left unresolved.

## 11. Working hypothesis test — ratios as invariants

**Hypothesis (P-001 refinement):**

> Reality Mechanics does not borrow physics as metaphor. It constrains relation, order, trace, carrying, pressure, rhythm, and availability until stable ratios appear. Those ratios may be the invariants. Physics then becomes the expressive language because it is the mature language of invariant behaviour.

**Test method:** Not whether RM "is physics." Whether **derived ratios behave like physical invariants** when **retraced** (same structure → same read) or **rendered** (observation transforms applied). Evidence: D-012 behaviour trace, `deriveFieldStatesPayload`, D-011 recurrence, renderer state variables.

### 11.1 What "invariant" means here

| Invariant class | Test |
|---|---|
| **Structure-invariant** | Same D1 structure + same config → same ratio on retrace (`GET /api/field/states`, `/api/field/behaviour-trace`) |
| **Observation-invariant** | Ratio unchanged under pan, zoom, focus change, reference frame, viewport resize |
| **Time-invariant** | Ratio unchanged across animation frames without structure/participant change |
| **Physics-analogue** | When rendered/traced, ratio produces behaviour resembling fields, gradients, propagation, equilibrium, resonance, or condensation **without requiring physics ontology** |

### 11.2 Structure-invariant ratios (the candidate invariants)

These **pass** the retrace test. They are the strongest evidence for the hypothesis.

| Ratio | Derivation constraint | Retrace stable? | Physics-analogue when rendered |
|---|---|---|---|
| `mass.carriers` | holds/traces in-degree only (`field-maturity.mjs` §3.3, locked) | **Yes** — integer from D1 scan; D-012 cites for every behaviour | Source **density** / deposition strength in pressure grids |
| `ratioMode` | Threshold on carriers (calibrated 3/8) | **Yes** — deterministic given thresholds | **Phase register** (discrete → transitional → continuous readability) |
| `transition`, `continuous`, `compression` | Client expansion from mode + x | **Yes** — pure function of state | Modulation depth / compression coefficients |
| `structuralMass` | carriers + ratio scalars + settledness + maturity | **Yes** — `structuralMassForState` is deterministic | **Effective mass** — condensation threshold, pulse depth, compression |
| Declared edge existence | Atlas structure | **Yes** — O-4: coupling only on declared edges | **Allowed propagation channels** only |
| Relation-type rhythm mode | holds/traces/carries/pairs/nests | **Yes** — per-type signature map in D-012 | Anchor / return / travel / answer / circulate — maps to tension, trajectory, propagation, resonance, enclosure |
| `settledness`, `maturityBand`, `agitation` | Time/revision/proposal reads | **Yes** at fixed timestamp | **Equilibrium-like** stability reads (settling → established → mature) |

**Finding:** The **scalar derived chain is invariant under retrace**. D-012 demonstrates this mechanically: `first.relation` with carriers=23 always yields structuralMass≈0.838, compression limits 5/3, same atlasSource fields — regardless of who observes or when the trace API is called (structure unchanged).

These are the programme's best candidates for **"the invariants"** — not because they are physical, but because they **survive constraint** and **determine render behaviour** downstream.

### 11.3 Observation-variant reads (not invariants — overlay layer)

These change under navigation, frame, viewport, or performance. They are **participant and instrument state**, not structure-derived invariants.

| Read | Varies with | Invariant? |
|---|---|---|
| `coupledSensibility` / frame alpha | Reference frame (`practice.ratio-read`, etc.) | **No** — 1.0 in-frame, 0.08 out (D-011 O-5) |
| Layout positions (`tx`, `ty`, `homePosition`) | Focus, viewport, hash jitter | **No** — deterministic per context, not per structure alone |
| `traversalMap`, `fieldMovementEvent` | Participant navigation history | **No** — accumulates; wake decays (~1.8s) |
| `relationPressureTraces` | Recent relation draws | **No** — exponential decay (`traceDecay`) |
| `relationTrajectoryMemory` | Smoothed Bézier controls | **No** — relaxes toward target each frame |
| `pressureField.values` (live) | Layout bounds, trace deposit, relaxation | **Partial** — target field is structure-driven; **displayed** field lerps (not instant equilibrium) |
| `homePressureField` | Viewport size (screen-space grid) | **No** — rebuilds on resize |
| `adaptiveAmbientScale` | Frame rate | **No** — performance overlay |
| Rhythm **phase** (`sin(time + phase)`) | Animation clock | **No** — time-dependent; **mode** is invariant |

**Finding:** The runtime correctly **separates** structure-invariant ratios (retraceable) from observation overlays (D-012 `runtimeOverlay` vs `atlasSource`). Physics language should attach to the **invariant layer** first; overlay behaviour should be labelled participant/instrument, not invariant.

### 11.4 Physics-behaviour correspondence under render/trace

Testing each physics-like behaviour against **whether it tracks an invariant ratio**:

| Physics-like behaviour | Tracks invariant ratio? | Evidence | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Field** (pressure membrane) | **Partial** | Deposition from `structuralMass` at positions; lattice is heuristic | **Behaves like** density field when grounded in mass; **not invariant** under viewport/transform |
| **Gradients** | **Partial** | `∇P` from deposited pressure; bends `fieldBend` | **Behaves like** gradient flow when P is mass-driven; derivative of non-invariant screen grid on home view |
| **Curvature** | **Weak** | `fieldBend` + relation `bowMult` | **Calibrated** response to gradient + type; not curvature from metric |
| **Conservation** | **Partial (structure only)** | Carrier count unchanged by observation; **no** conserved flux or energy | **Structure scalars conserved** under navigation; **no** dynamical conservation law |
| **Propagation** | **Yes (channel)** / **Heuristic (motion)** | Carry rhythm on declared `carries` edges only (D-011) | **Direction invariant** from relation type; bead motion is render heuristic |
| **Equilibrium** | **Partial** | Damped lerp to layout; `settledness`/`maturity` | **Approach to stable layout** resembles dissipative equilibrium; driven by invariant settledness |
| **Resonance** | **Partial** | Pair `reciprocity` oscillation; `ratioMode` modulates | **Rhythm mode invariant**; oscillation amplitude/heuristic |
| **Condensation** | **Yes (threshold)** | `structuralMass` gates condensation (O-001, D-011 O-1) | **Behaves like** phase condensation at mass threshold — strongest invariant-linked visual |

### 11.5 Constraints named in the hypothesis — do they produce stable ratios?

| Atlas constraint | Produces stable ratio? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Relation** | Indirect | Primitive; stability is edge **existence**, not a scalar |
| **Order** | Partial | `entry_order` → layout depth bands (O-7); visibility frame (P4), not mass |
| **Trace** | **Yes** | traces in-degree → `mass.carriers`; backward path availability |
| **Carrying** | **Yes (direction)** | carries edges + carry rhythm; forward channel invariant per edge |
| **Pressure** | **Unresolved** | Atlas term not in derivation chain; runtime pressure is **deposit heuristic**, not Atlas Pressure |
| **Rhythm** | **Yes (mode)** / **No (phase)** | Per-relation mode in `RHYTHM_SIGNATURES`; animation phase is time |
| **Availability** | **No ratio yet** | Atlas first-order term; no runtime permeability scalar (O-001 gap) |

### 11.6 Hypothesis verdict

| Clause | Result |
|---|---|
| RM does not borrow physics as **metaphor** | **Normatively yes; descriptively mixed.** Derived chain is non-metaphorical. `enginePhysics`, membrane flow, and pre-O-001 node rendering **are** metaphor layers (D-020B heuristic). Programme should **treat physics as expression of invariants only**. |
| Constraints produce **stable ratios** | **Supported** for holds/traces in-degree → mode → structuralMass chain. **Not yet** for availability, Atlas pressure, or rich structural signature. |
| Those ratios **may be the invariants** | **Supported with constraint.** They are **retrace-invariant** and **observation-invariant** for structure. They are **not** universal physical invariants (no conservation law, no coordinate-free field). They are **Reality Mechanics invariants**: what survives constraint and retrace. |
| Physics as **mature expressive language** | **Supported when mapped to invariant layer.** Condensation↔structuralMass, propagation↔carry channels, equilibrium↔settledness, field↔carrier density, resonance↔pair rhythm **mode**. **Fails** when physics vocabulary names overlay state (wake, frame dimming) or undervived Atlas terms (Pressure, Availability). |

**Refined verdict:** The hypothesis is **directionally correct** and **more precise than "RM is physics."** The derived ratios **do** behave like physical invariants **in the limited sense** that:

1. They are **stable under retrace** and **unchanged by how you look** (pan, zoom, frame).
2. When rendered, they produce **field-like, propagation-like, equilibrium-like, and condensation-like** behaviours **proportionally** — not decoratively.
3. Physics is **appropriate expressive language** for **this invariant layer**, not for the whole Observatory.

**What the hypothesis does not yet cover:** Participant-history overlays, calibrated thresholds, heuristic amplification, and the gap between Atlas Pressure/Availability and runtime field machinery. Those remain **non-invariant** or **unresolved** — and physics language applied there would be **metaphor**, which the hypothesis correctly rejects.

### 11.7 Implication for programme posture

```text
Constraint (Atlas) → stable ratios (invariants under retrace)
        ↓
Physics vocabulary → expressive render/trace language for those ratios only
        ↓
Participant overlay → labelled separately (not invariant, not physics-claimed)
```

O-001 and D-012 already embody this stack. Future commissions (P-002–P-007) should **extend the invariant layer** before adding more physics expression.
