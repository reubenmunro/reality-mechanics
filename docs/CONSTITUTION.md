# Reality Mechanics Constitution

## Purpose

This Constitution records the governing constraints of the Reality Mechanics repository.

It is not the Atlas.

It is not the Calculus.

It is not the Practice.

It is not the Platform.

It defines what must remain preserved while those bodies develop, change, and correct themselves.

### Constitutional Principle

This Constitution does not attempt to establish Reality Mechanics.

It establishes the conditions under which Reality Mechanics may continue to establish itself.

### Constitutional Responsibility

The Constitution preserves the standard of care for Reality Mechanics.

It does not preserve any particular implementation.

Implementations may be replaced.

The discipline by which they are evaluated should remain consistent.

---

## Constitutional Aim
Reality Mechanics exists to establish, steward, and serve a retraceable body of structural reads whose derivations remain independently reviewable.

Every accepted claim should be independently reviewable.

Every accepted decision should be independently retraceable.

Every accepted transformation should either preserve the order it carries into its next readable form, or explicitly identify where that preservation remains unresolved.

---

## Constitutional Scope

This Constitution governs constraints, not conclusions.

It establishes how Reality Mechanics preserves authority while its Atlas, Calculus, Practice, and Platform continue to develop.

It does not establish Atlas terms.

It does not establish Calculus operations.

It does not settle architectural investigations.

Architectural investigations may inform the Constitution but are not themselves constitutional content.

Its role is to preserve the conditions under which those bodies may be independently derived, reviewed, and corrected.

### Constitutional Test

A statement belongs in this Constitution only if removing it would weaken the repository's ability to govern itself responsibly.

If removing the statement changes only implementation, workflow, or explanation, it belongs elsewhere.

---

## Constitutional Character

This Constitution is intentionally conservative.

Its purpose is not to accelerate development.

Its purpose is to preserve the structural conditions under which Reality Mechanics may continue to correct itself.

A constitutional constraint should outlast implementations, workflows, technologies, AI models, and repository structure.

### Constitutional Discipline

The Constitution should contain only governing constraints and authority.

It should not become a repository for derivations, implementation guidance, workflows, or architectural exploration.

Those belong in the Atlas, Calculus, Practice, or Architectural Notes.

---

## Authority Order

The authority order describes governance.

It should not be interpreted as a derivation sequence or an implementation dependency.

This section describes authority, not derivation.

Authority determines what may govern.

It does not determine what gives rise to what.

Reality Mechanics recognises the following authority order:

```text
Reality
↓
Atlas
↓
Calculus
↓
Practice
↓
Platform
↓
Reports
```

Authority flows downward.

Evidence flows upward.

Interpretation flows upward for review.

Authority never flows upward.

No lower body may redefine the authority of a higher body.

Authority should not be confused with derivation.

A lower body may reveal evidence requiring a higher body to correct itself.

Correction occurs through review, not by reversing authority.

Authority is exercised through stewardship.

Authority is strengthened by successful correction, not by resisting it.

Reports discover and preserve evidence. They do not create authority by themselves.

The Platform serves the Atlas. It does not become the Atlas.

The Practice governs stewardship. It does not redefine the Atlas.

The Calculus derives operations. It does not invent them by preference.

The Atlas preserves accepted structural reads. It remains subordinate to reality.

---

### Reading the Constitution

Each constitutional constraint should answer one question:

> What must remain true for Reality Mechanics to continue correcting itself?

If a statement does not answer that question, it probably belongs elsewhere.

## Constitutional Constraints

### C001 — Repository Evidence Over Memory

Repository evidence has authority over previous conversations, AI memory, preference, assumption, or convenience.

### C002 — Retraceability

Every accepted claim, decision, and transformation should remain independently retraceable.

Where retraceability is absent, the claim remains provisional.

Retraceability applies equally to evidence, derivations, operations, decisions, and amendments.

### C003 — Atlas Authority

The Atlas is authoritative for accepted Reality Mechanics terms and structural reads.

Practice documents, reports, and architectural notes may propose interpretations, but they do not change Atlas authority.

The Atlas records accepted structural reads. It does not record every investigation.

### C004 — Calculus Authority

The Calculus derives and justifies operations and transformations.

It is concerned with what occurs between states, not merely the names of states.

### C004A — Derivation Before Promotion

No proposed term, operation, relation, or governing principle should be promoted to constitutional, calculative, or Atlas authority before its derivation has been independently reviewed.

Useful hypotheses remain hypotheses until their carrying has been retraced.

### C005 — Practice Authority

The Practice governs how work is commissioned, contracted, evidenced, reviewed, and resolved.

Practice documents describe the current governed way of working. They are not the Practice Calculus unless their transformations have been derived from the Atlas.

### C006 — Platform Authority

The Platform presents, serves, indexes, and carries Reality Mechanics.

It must remain subordinate to the Atlas and Practice.

It must not become the source of structural truth.

### C007 — Reports as Evidence

Reports preserve evidence gathered under contract.

A report may enable a decision, but it does not itself decide.

### C008 — Unknowns Remain Unknown

An unknown must be preserved as unknown until evidence changes its state.

Invented certainty violates this Constitution.

### C009 — Relation Over Percentage

Where possible, repository value and readiness should be expressed through structural relations rather than invented percentages.

Examples:

```text
checked : total
known : unknown
blocked : unblocked
verified : assumed
reproducible : environment-dependent
```

### C010 — Operations Are Accountable

Where `:` is used as an operator, the operation must remain accountable to derivation.

A named sequence is not enough.

The transformation between terms must become retraceable.

An operation should not be accepted because it appears useful.

It should be accepted because its carrying can be independently retraced.

### C011 — Separation of Investigation and Authority

An investigation may influence authority.

It does not become authority until accepted through the appropriate governing body.

Architectural notes, reports, and practice investigations remain proposals until promoted through accepted repository processes.

### C012 — Constitutional Minimality

The Constitution should remain as small as possible while preserving the governing discipline.

If a statement can live with equal authority in the Atlas, Calculus, Practice, Platform, or Architectural Notes, it should not be promoted into the Constitution.

### C013 — Corrigibility

Every accepted body of Reality Mechanics must remain corrigible through evidence.

No accepted body is immune from correction.

The Constitution protects the process of correction, not the permanence of conclusions.

### C014 — Constitutional Restraint

The Constitution shall not contain unresolved architectural investigations.

Working investigations belong in Architectural Notes until they have been derived, reviewed, and accepted.

Only governing constraints may be promoted into this document.

## Stewardship Standard

Stewardship protects the Constitution by applying it rather than expanding it.

A change should not be accepted because it is useful, elegant, convenient, persuasive, or widely agreed.

A change should be accepted when it is:

- evidence-backed,
- bounded,
- retraceable,
- structurally accountable,
- and accepted by the appropriate authority.

---

## AI Standard of Care

AI systems may assist with investigation, drafting, implementation, and review.

AI systems do not create authority by generating language.

AI output must be treated as a read, not as authority.

The standard question for AI output is:

> Can this read be retraced to the structure it claims to carry?

A useful read without a retraceable carrying remains provisional.

AI contributes interpretation.

Stewardship determines authority.

---

## Amendment Standard

This Constitution should change only when doing so increases structural accountability without reducing retraceability.

Amendments should be made only when a governing constraint has become clearer, more accurate, or more structurally necessary.

A constitutional amendment should identify:

- what pressure required the change,
- what evidence supports the change,
- what existing constraint is clarified, revised, or replaced,
- and what authority accepts the amendment.

Constitutional amendments should normally follow successful derivation elsewhere in the repository rather than lead it.

## Current Status

The Constitution is accepted as the governing constitutional document for the repository.

It records current governing constraints.

It also records the standard of care by which those constraints may themselves be revised.

It does not complete the Atlas, Calculus, Practice, or Platform.
It exists to preserve structural accountability while allowing every accepted body of Reality Mechanics to remain corrigible through evidence and retraceable derivation.

### Constitutional Maturity

The Constitution is now considered a stable governing document.

Future development effort should preferentially occur within the Atlas, Calculus, Practice, Platform, or Architectural Notes.

Constitutional change should normally arise only when those bodies demonstrate that an existing governing constraint is incomplete, inaccurate, or insufficient.

### Constitutional Boundary

The Constitution intentionally stops here.

Questions of derivation belong to the Calculus.

Questions of accepted structural reads belong to the Atlas.

Questions of operational stewardship belong to the Practice.

Questions of implementation belong to the Platform.

Future architectural discoveries should normally mature within those bodies before being considered for constitutional promotion.

---

## Closing Principle

Reality Mechanics remains corrigible.

No document within the repository is protected from correction.

What is protected is the standard of care by which correction occurs.

The Constitution therefore protects retraceability, authority, and derivation before it protects any particular conclusion.

Its purpose is not to prevent change.

Its purpose is to ensure that change remains accountable.

The Constitution protects the discipline before it protects the repository.

The repository may change.

The standard of care by which it changes should endure.


The highest constitutional success is not a document that never changes.

It is a discipline that preserves its standard of care while remaining capable of responsible correction.

The Constitution therefore measures its success by what it prevents itself from containing.

Its strength lies in preserving clear authority, clear boundaries, and a disciplined path for correction.

---

A mature Constitution becomes quieter over time.

As Reality Mechanics matures, growth should occur primarily within the Atlas and Calculus, while the Constitution changes only when a governing constraint has been conclusively demonstrated.
