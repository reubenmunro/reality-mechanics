# Reality Mechanics Delivery Plan

## Mission

Reality Mechanics exists to increase structural perception.

Every task, investigation, implementation and feature must contribute to that mission.

---

# Delivery Principle

Every Cursor commission must finish with a tangible deliverable before context or tokens are exhausted.

The programme does not aim to produce a final doctrine. It prepares reviewable submissions.

Accepted deliverables include:
- Evidence report
- Characterisation
- Architectural paper
- Atlas refinement
- Practice refinement
- Calculus refinement
- Theory refinement
- Proof refinement
- Website implementation
- Calibration implementation

Never end a session with only discussion.

---

# Architectural Governance

Every commission must:

- contribute to the mission;
- produce a persisted deliverable;
- remain retraceable to evidence;
- minimise architectural drift;
- pass the [discovery-to-implementation gate](docs/practice/DISCOVERY_TO_IMPLEMENTATION.md) when proposing Atlas, runtime, or Working Architecture changes.

The Lead Architect reviews every deliverable and records one outcome:

- Accepted
- Deferred
- Rejected

Only accepted deliverables become part of the Observatory programme.

---

# Continuous Delivery Cycle

1. Detect structural pressure.
2. Commission a narrowly scoped investigation.
3. Produce a persisted deliverable.
4. Characterise accumulated evidence.
5. Architect review.
6. Accept, reject or defer.

If accepted:

7. Promote the accepted findings to the programme's Working Architecture.
8. Propagate the Working Architecture through:
   - Atlas
   - Practice
   - Calculus
   - Theory
   - Proof
   - Calibration
   - Observatory
9. Prepare the next reviewable submission.
10. Repeat.

# Submission Model

The Observatory is the public medium for reviewable submissions.

A submission is not a claim of final certainty.

A submission is a coordinated package that exposes:

- the current Working Architecture;
- the evidence supporting it;
- unresolved questions;
- accepted deliverables;
- deferred or rejected alternatives;
- the next required work.

The public website should allow independent participants to review, challenge and retrace the programme in the same way a consent authority reviews a coordinated building consent package.

Reports function as supporting evidence.

The Observatory functions as the submission medium.

# Definition of Done

A commission is complete only when:

- a persisted deliverable exists;
- evidence has been recorded;
- accumulated evidence has been characterised where relevant;
- retraceability is preserved;
- architectural review has been completed;
- accepted findings have been promoted or explicitly held back;
- the next submission or commission has been identified.
